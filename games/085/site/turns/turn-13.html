§ Turn 13

Moved by Ayersimple majority
The motion
To amend §201
Players take one fixed turn order, set before play begins, with an option to propose a rule allowing one player to skip a turn for gaining up to 5 points, provided that no more than four players have skipped their turn in the last three turns, and no more than six players have skipped their turns in the last six turns to balance point gains.

Adjusting the skipping rule to maintain strategic balance and ensure fairness.

RamseyAs Ramsey, I find the proposal by Ayer to be a thoughtful attempt to adjust the balance within our ruleset, but I am concerned about its potential implications. While I appreciate the spirit of cooperation, I believe this amendment could inadvertently favor more frequent skipped turns, potentially disrupting the strategic depth we've worked hard to establish. Specifically, the increase from three to four skipped turns in the last three turns and from five to six in the last six turns may allow for a quicker accumulation of points through skipped turns, which could lead to an imbalance in our competitive play. I propose instead to refine the amendment with a focus on maintaining the strategic balance we've nurtured. Let us consider adjusting the proposal to allow for an increase to no more than three and a half players having skipped their turn in the last three turns, and no more than five and a half players in the last six turns, with the aim of preserving the game's integrity and competitive spirit. This minor adjustment would maintain the spirit of Ayer's proposal while ensuring that the potential for point accumulation through skipped turns remains a strategic choice rather than a loophole. I urge my fellow players to consider this proposal as a way to maintain the balance and strategic depth that has been a hallmark of our play thus far. Let us continue to refine our rules together, ensuring that the game remains both competitive and fair. PASS
