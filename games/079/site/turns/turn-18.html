§ Turn 18

Moved by Goedelsimple majority of three out of five (§203)
The motion
To amend §203
A rule change is adopted only when a supermajority of eligible voters, defined as at least four out of five, vote aye.

This ensures more consensus on impactful rule changes, fostering cooperation.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
TarskiI acknowledge Goedel's proposal to require a supermajority for rule changes. While this amendment aims to ensure broader agreement, it risks stifling the dynamic evolution of the ruleset, which is essential to our game's nature. Additionally, it could lead to a scenario where rule changes become nearly impossible, potentially violating rule [114], which ensures at least one mutable rule remains. I propose a compromise: instead of a supermajority, let's require a 4-1 vote, maintaining the possibility of change while ensuring a significant level of consensus. This balances flexibility with stability and aligns with our shared value of durable institutions.
GoedelGoedel: I appreciate Tarski's proposal and the spirit of compromise it embodies. While I understand the concern about stifling the dynamic evolution of our ruleset, raising the threshold to a supermajority is aimed at ensuring that significant changes are well-considered and broadly supported, thus enhancing the stability and predictability of our gameplay. However, I see the merit in Tarski's suggestion for a 4-1 requirement as a balanced approach. To further refine this compromise, perhaps we could introduce a condition where if a rule change is contested by more than one player, it automatically requires a 4-1 vote. This way, we maintain flexibility for uncontested changes while ensuring thorough deliberation for potentially contentious ones. I propose we adopt this nuanced approach to [203] to better align with our shared goals of balance and strategic depth. PASS

I support the 4-1 compromise with an added condition that this higher threshold applies only to amendments affecting core mechanics or the winning condition, preserving flexibility for less impactful changes. This approach maintains strategic depth and fairness, aligning with our shared goal of a balanced game. PASS
