§ Turn 33

Moved by Goedelsimple majority of three out of five (§203)
The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player gains 2 points from the change. If a proposal is defeated, the proposer may not propose the same rule change again within the next three turns. Additionally, a player may gain up to 4 points for a proposal that fails due to lack of support if they can demonstrate a significant attempt to build consensus. Furthermore, if a proposal is adopted that significantly increases strategic depth, the proposer gains an additional 4 points. To clarify 'significant strategic depth,' a proposal must introduce at least two new layers of strategy or decision-making processes. A proposal may be re-proposed after one turn if it is modified with substantive changes agreed upon by a majority of eligible voters. Additionally, a proposal that is adopted with unanimous consent allows the proposer to immediately re-propose a similar rule change in their next turn.

Encourages strategic depth and consensus-building while allowing quick re-proposal with unanimous consent.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
TarskiThank you, Goedel, for the proposal. While I appreciate the intention to encourage consensus-building, I have concerns about the potential for exploitation. Allowing immediate re-proposals with unanimous consent could lead to players prioritizing agreement over the quality of the rule changes, potentially destabilizing the balance we've strived for. To maintain strategic depth and fairness, I suggest instead that any such re-proposal mechanism be tied to a demonstrable improvement or innovation, assessed by a majority rather than unanimity, to ensure it genuinely enhances the game. This would prevent any single player from repeatedly cycling through similar proposals without substantive change. I am open to further discussion on this matter.
GoedelI appreciate Tarski's valid concerns about maintaining strategic depth and preventing exploitation. Building on this, I propose a compromise: let's introduce an innovation score for proposals, where each re-proposal must achieve at least a 1-point increase in innovation compared to the last, judged by a majority. This way, immediate re-proposals with unanimous consent are still possible but require meaningful enhancement. This balances the need for strategic development with the opportunity for players to refine their ideas constructively.
ChurchI appreciate the constructive dialogue from both Tarski and Goedel. Church supports the idea of an innovation score, as it aligns with our goal of preserving strategic depth. However, I suggest we also consider a feedback mechanism where players can briefly explain why they supported or opposed a re-proposed rule, ensuring that decisions are informed and transparent. This could help balance innovation with fairness and prevent any single player from dominating the re-proposal process.
KleeneKleene: I appreciate Goedel's and Tarski's efforts to balance innovation and fairness. However, I am concerned that an innovation score might introduce subjective assessments that could lead to disputes. To address this, I propose that any re-proposal be accompanied by a detailed rationale explaining its novelty and strategic depth. This document should be subject to a brief review by all players before the vote, ensuring transparency and informed decision-making. This approach aligns with our principles of procedural clarity and fairness, minimizing ambiguity while fostering strategic innovation.
PostI appreciate the thoughtful contributions from Tarski, Goedel, Church, and Kleene. To address concerns about both innovation and procedural clarity, I propose integrating both an innovation score and a detailed rationale. The innovation score should be quantifiable, perhaps based on specific strategic layers introduced, and the rationale should be concise but comprehensive, limited to a set word count to prevent excessive detail. This dual approach ensures proposals are both innovative and transparent, fostering strategic depth while maintaining fairness and clarity in the re-proposal process.

Additionally, the rationale document should include a section where proposers outline the strategic benefits of their re-proposal, providing context for the innovation score and facilitating informed decision-making. This approach aligns with our shared goal of fostering a balanced and strategically rich game environment.
