The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player gains 2 points from the change. If a proposal is defeated, the proposer may not propose the same rule change again within the next three turns. Additionally, a player may gain up to 6 points for a proposal that fails due to lack of support if they can demonstrate a significant attempt to build consensus. Furthermore, if a proposal is adopted that significantly increases strategic depth, the proposer gains an additional 4 points. To clarify 'significant strategic depth,' a proposal must introduce at least three new layers of strategy or decision-making processes. A proposal may be re-proposed after one turn if it is modified with substantive changes agreed upon by a majority of eligible voters. Additionally, a proposal that is adopted with unanimous consent allows the proposer to immediately re-propose a similar rule change in their next turn. To promote strategic engagement, if a player's cumulative score reaches a multiple of 10, they gain the ability to propose an additional rule change in their next turn. This additional proposal is subject to the same conditions as a regular proposal. Moreover, players must provide a brief rationale for their vote on each proposal, which is shared publicly, to enhance transparency and accountability in the decision-making process.

Enhancing strategic transparency and accountability by requiring public rationales for votes.

TarskiI appreciate the spirit of transparency and accountability in this proposal. However, I have concerns that requiring a public rationale for every vote could lead to excessive deliberation and potentially strategic voting behavior, undermining the fluidity and strategic depth we aim to promote. To address this, I suggest a compromise: instead of mandating a rationale for every vote, we could require a brief rationale only for votes that diverge from the majority, or when a rule change significantly impacts the game dynamics. This would still enhance transparency where it matters most, while preserving the efficiency and strategic nuance of our voting process. In light of this, I propose to amend the new provision as follows: "Players must provide a brief rationale for their vote on each proposal when their vote diverges from the majority or when the proposal significantly impacts the game dynamics. This rationale is shared publicly to enhance transparency and accountability in the decision-making process." This way, we balance the need for transparency with the need to maintain a strategic and efficient game environment. I seek support for this amended proposal.
