§ Turn 17

Moved by Tarskisimple majority of three out of five (§203)
The motion
To amend §206
An adopted rule change takes effect immediately after the vote, but the proposer may gain points from the change in their next turn.

Facilitates strategic planning while balancing immediate effects and player opportunities.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
TarskiI appreciate Goedel's effort in setting a dynamic target score. However, adjusting the target score alone does not fully address the imbalance in strategic opportunities among players with differing scores. My proposal to allow the proposer to gain points from an adopted change in their next turn ensures a more immediate reward for successful rule changes, incentivizing active participation and fostering a competitive environment. This amendment also maintains fairness by applying to all proposers equally. I invite others to consider these points and support this balanced approach.
GoedelGoedel: I appreciate Tarski's focus on balancing strategic opportunities. However, immediate application of the change could lead to rushed or less considered proposals, potentially destabilizing the game. To foster thoughtful rule changes, I propose a slight delay before points are gained, ensuring players have ample time to reflect and strategize. This compromise encourages both active participation and careful decision-making. Let's aim for a balanced approach that rewards initiative without sacrificing the game's integrity.
ChurchChurch: I appreciate both Tarski's and Goedel's perspectives. To balance immediate rewards with thoughtful deliberation, I propose a middle ground: allow the proposer to gain points in their next turn, but introduce a brief, fixed reflection period after the vote before the points are counted. This encourages strategic thinking while maintaining fairness and motivation for all players.
KleeneKleene: I appreciate the intentions behind Tarski's and Goedel's proposals, but to ensure clarity and procedural integrity, I propose a precise condition: a proposer can gain points from their own successful change only if another player has proposed a rule change in their last turn. This balances strategic depth with fairness and prevents consecutive self-benefiting rule changes. Let's maintain procedural rigor while enhancing strategic engagement.
PostPost: I appreciate the collaborative spirit in these proposals. While immediate point rewards encourage engagement, a reflection period could enhance strategic depth, as Church suggests. However, to prevent exploitation, I support Kleene's condition: points should be gained only if another player proposed a change in their last turn. Let's ensure fairness and strategic balance by adopting this amendment.
