§ Turn 15

Moved by Tarskisimple majority of three out of five (§203)
The motion
To amend §206
An adopted rule change takes effect immediately after the vote, and the proposer may gain points from the change in their next turn.

Enable proposers to benefit from their successful amendments.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
TarskiI appreciate the proposal, Tarski, for its intention to reward creativity and risk in rule changes. However, allowing proposers to immediately gain points could lead to an imbalance, incentivizing changes that benefit the proposer disproportionately. I suggest a middle ground: let the proposer gain points after one full round of play, ensuring their rule change has a fair impact on the game before reaping benefits. This maintains strategic depth and fairness.
GoedelGoedel: I appreciate Tarski's concerns about balance and Tarski's suggestion for fairness. To build on this, we could introduce a condition where the proposer gains points only if the change benefits all players equally in the next round, ensuring it aligns with collective interests before rewarding the proposer. This approach fosters strategic creativity while maintaining game integrity.
ChurchChurch: I appreciate the focus on maintaining balance and fairness in Tarski's and Goedel's suggestions. To ensure that rule changes don't inadvertently create advantage for the proposer, I propose that any rule change allowing immediate point gain should be reviewed by all players in the next round for fairness before points are awarded. This adds a layer of collective oversight, aligning individual creativity with the common good.
KleeneKleene: I appreciate the thoughtful considerations from Tarski, Goedel, and Church. To align with our principles of fairness and procedural integrity, I propose a modification: allow the proposer to gain points after the next two complete rounds of play. This ensures the rule change has a fair impact on the game dynamics before any rewards are distributed, supporting strategic depth without compromising balance.
PostPost: I appreciate the thoughtful dialogue from Tarski, Goedel, Church, and Kleene. To support strategic innovation while ensuring balance, I suggest implementing a conditional point gain: the proposer can earn points if the change leads to at least one successful rule proposal by another player in the next round. This encourages creative yet cooperative rule-making, rewarding proposers only when their change positively influences the game for others.
Discussion · round 2
TarskiTarski: I appreciate the insights and suggestions from Goedel, Church, Kleene, and Post. To ensure strategic balance and fairness, I propose a synthesis: allow point gain after one full round, provided a majority of players, including those who voted against, affirm the change's fairness. This approach maintains strategic depth while ensuring broad consensus on rule impacts, aligning individual innovation with collective fairness.
GoedelGoedel: I appreciate the synthesis proposed by Tarski, which balances immediate benefits with a fairness check. To further enhance this, I suggest adding a provision: if the rule change is deemed unfair by the majority, the proposer not only loses the potential point gain but also must propose a compensatory amendment in the subsequent turn. This ensures accountability and continued alignment with our goal of fair play.
ChurchChurch: I appreciate the synthesis and accountability measures proposed by Tarski and Goedel. To further ensure that rule changes benefit the broader game dynamics, I propose adding a clause that requires a brief review period after one round, where players can propose minor adjustments to the new rule if necessary. This ensures that any unforeseen imbalances can be promptly addressed, maintaining both strategic depth and fairness throughout the game.
KleeneKleene: I appreciate the synthesis from Tarski, Goedel, and Church. To further ensure procedural fairness, I propose adding that the review and adjustment period be facilitated by an impartial observer, perhaps rotating among players, to maintain objectivity. This would support fair play by providing unbiased oversight on rule impacts, ensuring all players' interests are considered.
PostPost: I appreciate the collaborative spirit in Kleene's suggestion of an impartial observer to oversee fairness. To enhance this, let's ensure the observer role rotates systematically among players to avoid bias and maintain transparency. This rotation can be scheduled at the start of each round, fostering trust and accountability in our evolving ruleset.
Discussion · round 3
TarskiTarski: I appreciate Kleene's and Post's contributions to maintaining fairness and transparency with the observer system. To further align with our goal of equitable game dynamics, I suggest that if the impartial observer determines a rule change to be imbalanced, the proposer must collaborate with the opposing players to devise a compensatory rule that restores balance before points can be awarded. This promotes cooperative problem-solving and ensures all players contribute to a fair gaming environment.
GoedelGoedel: I appreciate the collaborative framework proposed by Tarski, Kleene, and Post. To ensure fairness and strategic integrity, I suggest that the compensatory rule, if needed, be co-authored by the proposer and a majority of players who opposed the original change. This encourages consensus-building and reinforces the principle that rule modifications should benefit the entire game community.

To further ensure strategic integrity and fairness, I suggest that if a compensatory rule is needed, it should also include a mechanism for periodic review to assess its impact over multiple rounds. This ensures that any adjustments remain aligned with the evolving game dynamics and continue to support the common good.
KleeneKleene: I appreciate the commitment to fairness and strategic integrity in Tarski's, Goedel's, Church's, and Post's proposals. To ensure that rule changes and compensatory rules are both fair and effective, I propose that any periodic review mechanism include a feedback loop where players can anonymously submit concerns about game balance before each review, ensuring that all voices are heard and considered in maintaining the game's integrity.

To further bolster this framework, I propose that the feedback loop for periodic reviews includes a structured format for players to suggest potential improvements or clarifications. This would ensure that feedback is actionable and contributes constructively to refining the game dynamics, maintaining a fair and engaging experience for all players.
