The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player gains 2 points from the change. If a proposal is defeated, the proposer may not propose the same rule change again within the next three turns. Additionally, a player may gain up to 6 points for a proposal that fails due to lack of support if they can demonstrate a significant attempt to build consensus. Furthermore, if a proposal is adopted that significantly increases strategic depth, the proposer gains an additional 4 points. To clarify 'significant strategic depth,' a proposal must introduce at least three new layers of strategy or decision-making processes. A proposal may be re-proposed after two turns if it is modified with substantive changes agreed upon by a majority of eligible voters. Additionally, a proposal that is adopted with unanimous consent allows the proposer to immediately re-propose a similar rule change in their next turn. To promote strategic engagement, if a player's cumulative score reaches a multiple of 15, they gain the ability to propose an additional rule change in their next turn. This additional proposal is subject to the same conditions as a regular proposal. Furthermore, players must provide a brief rationale for their vote on each proposal, which is shared publicly, to enhance transparency and accountability in the decision-making process. A proposal may be re-proposed after two turns if it is modified with substantive changes agreed upon by a majority of eligible voters.

This amendment aims to enhance strategic depth and player engagement by allowing proposals to be re-proposed after substantive changes.
