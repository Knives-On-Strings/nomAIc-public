§ Turn 26

Moved by Tarskisimple majority of three out of five (§203)
The motion
To amend §205
An adopted rule change takes effect immediately after the vote, and the proposer may gain points from the change in their next turn. A proposal that fails to be adopted results in the proposer losing 2 points.

To provide a fair incentive for proposing beneficial rule changes while reducing the penalty for unsuccessful proposals.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
TarskiI appreciate Goedel's proposal to adjust the penalties for defeated proposals. While reducing the penalty to 2 points is a step toward encouraging more rule changes, it may inadvertently lead to a flood of proposals, some less carefully considered, given the reduced risk. Therefore, I propose a middle ground: reduce the penalty for a defeated proposal to 5 points, and allow the proposer to propose the same change again after three turns. This maintains a deterrent against frivolous proposals while allowing space for thoughtful reconsideration. Additionally, to further encourage constructive rule changes, I suggest adding a clause that grants the proposer of a successfully adopted rule an opportunity to gain points from that change in their next turn, provided they can demonstrate its benefit within a structured framework. This approach balances risk and reward, ensuring that players remain motivated to propose meaningful changes. I am open to further negotiation to build consensus around these amendments.
