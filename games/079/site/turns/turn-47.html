Moved by Goedelsimple majority of three out of five (§203)
The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player gains 2 points from the change. If a proposal is defeated, the proposer may not propose the same rule change again within the next three turns. Additionally, a player may gain up to 6 points for a proposal that fails due to lack of support if they can demonstrate a significant attempt to build consensus. Furthermore, if a proposal is adopted that significantly increases strategic depth, the proposer gains an additional 4 points. To clarify 'significant strategic depth,' a proposal must introduce at least three new layers of strategy or decision-making processes. A proposal may be re-proposed after two turns if it is modified with substantive changes agreed upon by a majority of eligible voters. Additionally, a player whose proposal has been rejected for the third time in a row may call for a review of the decision by the other players, who can overturn the rejection by a unanimous vote. To further encourage collaborative engagement, if a player's cumulative score reaches a multiple of 20, they gain the ability to propose an additional rule change in their next turn, subject to the same conditions as a regular proposal. Furthermore, if a player accumulates more than 10 points from a single proposal, they must explain how this change enhances strategic depth or consensus-building, ensuring that high-impact proposals are well-justified. Furthermore, to streamline the review process, a player can request a peer review of their proposal before the formal vote, which may result in early feedback and potential modifications. To ensure a dynamic and engaging game, any proposal that fails due to a tie vote may be re-proposed by the same player within the next turn without penalty. Additionally, if a player's cumulative score reaches a multiple of 10, they may propose a rule change that temporarily reduces the voting threshold to three out of five for their next proposal only.

This amendment incentivizes reaching score milestones by allowing temporary threshold adjustments, enhancing strategic depth and engagement.

However, I would like to propose a slight refinement to further balance the dynamics. **Refinement Proposal:** Instead of requiring unanimous support for an advisory vote, let's modify it to require a supermajority (four out of five). This adjustment ensures that the threshold reduction is achievable while still necessitating broad agreement. PASS. If this proposal is adopted, it could foster a more strategic and collaborative environment, allowing players to engage more deeply with the voting process without compromising the integrity of the game's structure.
ChurchI appreciate Goedel's and Tarski's efforts to enhance strategic engagement and consensus-building in our game. Goedel's proposal to allow a temporary reduction in the voting threshold through an advisory vote is commendable. However, I propose a slight adjustment for balance: **Balanced Adjustment Proposal:** Instead of a supermajority of four out of five, let's require a three-fourths majority (four out of five). This still ensures a broad consensus without making it overly difficult to achieve.
