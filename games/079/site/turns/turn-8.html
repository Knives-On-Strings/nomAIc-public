§ Turn 8

Moved by Goedelsimple majority (§203)
The motion
To amend §203
A rule change is adopted only when a supermajority of eligible voters, defined as at least four out of five, vote aye.

This amendment aims to balance the ease of rule adoption with the need for broader consensus to ensure stability and fairness in the game.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
TarskiWhile I appreciate Goedel's proposal to raise the adoption threshold to a supermajority, I must express concern that it could stifle productive rule changes, particularly in our early game phases. I propose instead to refine our rule by introducing a sliding scale: a simple majority is required initially, but as players' scores increase, the threshold gradually rises to a supermajority. This ensures a balance between flexibility and consensus as the game progresses.
GoedelGoedel: Tarski, I appreciate your concern about stifling productive rule changes. Let's build on your idea by setting specific score milestones where the threshold increases: at 20 points, 40 points, and finally at 60 points, the threshold reaches a supermajority. This way, we maintain flexibility early on while ensuring broader consensus as the game intensifies.
ChurchChurch: I appreciate both Goedel and Tarski's efforts to balance flexibility with consensus. However, I suggest a different approach: let's tie the threshold increase not to individual scores but to collective milestones—such as every 50 points collectively accumulated by all players. This way, we ensure that the game's evolving dynamics drive the need for broader agreement, rather than individual scoring, which can vary widely among players.
KleeneKleene: I appreciate Church's suggestion to base the threshold on collective milestones, as it aligns with the principle of considering the group's dynamics. To ensure fairness, let's propose the threshold increase at cumulative scores of 50, 100, and 150 points, transitioning from a simple majority to a supermajority. This gradual approach respects both individual and collective progress, maintaining procedural integrity.
PostPost: I appreciate the thoughtful contributions from all sides. This encourages collaboration and innovation while maintaining the integrity of our evolving rule system.
