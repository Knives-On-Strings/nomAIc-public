§ Turn 28

Moved by Tarskisimple majority of three out of five (§203)
The motion
To amend §203
A rule change is adopted only when a unanimous vote of all eligible voters is achieved. A proposal affecting the voting threshold itself requires unanimous approval.

Strengthening the decision-making process ensures consensus and prevents hasty rule changes.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
TarskiThank you, Tarski, for your proposal to amend rule 203. I appreciate your intention to promote consensus in rule changes. However, I must express some concerns. Requiring unanimous approval for all rule changes could significantly slow down our game, making it difficult to adapt to evolving dynamics. Additionally, it risks allowing a single player to veto changes they disagree with, potentially stalling progress altogether. To address your concerns about ensuring thorough consensus, I propose a compromise: maintain the simple majority for most changes but require a supermajority (4 out of 5) specifically for rule changes impacting voting thresholds or other fundamental aspects. This approach balances the need for consensus with the ability to proceed efficiently. I am open to further discussion and negotiation to refine this proposal and ensure it aligns with our shared goal of a fair and dynamic game.
GoedelThank you, Tarski, for your thoughtful proposal. I appreciate the focus on reaching consensus, and I understand the concern that requiring unanimous votes could stifle progress. Your suggestion of a supermajority for critical rule changes is a constructive step towards balancing consensus with flexibility. To further enhance this proposal, I suggest adding a clause that allows for periodic review of the supermajority requirement. If the game dynamics change or players feel the supermajority is hindering progress, we can revisit this threshold. This ensures we maintain adaptability while still respecting the importance of consensus on fundamental changes. I look forward to collaborating on refining this proposal to reflect our collective goals.
ChurchThank you, Tarski and Goedel, for your thoughtful contributions. I appreciate the proposal's intent to ensure consensus while recognizing the potential for stalling progress. I support the idea of a supermajority for critical rule changes, as it strikes a balance between thorough consensus and flexibility. This could prevent indefinite delays while maintaining fairness and collective decision-making. I am open to further discussions to refine these aspects and ensure we have a robust and adaptable rule-change process.

PostThank you, Tarski, Goedel, and Church, for your insightful contributions. I appreciate the emphasis on ensuring both consensus and progress. The supermajority idea is a step in the right direction, and I am particularly intrigued by the suggestion of a periodic review to adapt to changing dynamics. To complement these ideas, I propose adding a temporary "provisional rule" mechanism. If a supermajority is not reached, the proposed rule can be adopted on a trial basis for a limited number of turns. This allows us to assess its impact without committing to a permanent change. This trial period could then be subject to review and either adopted or rejected with a simple majority, ensuring flexibility and responsiveness to the game's evolving needs. I am open to further refining these ideas to reach a consensus that benefits all players. Let's work together to craft a rule change process that is both fair and dynamic.
