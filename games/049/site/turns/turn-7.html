Moved by Xunziunanimous consent to transmute (§109)
The motion
To transmute §202
and reclassify it between entrenched and mutable.

To preserve the potential for strategic depth while maintaining the current structure, we should make the rules regarding turn scoring mutable to allow for adjustments based on game progress.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
XunziHanFeizi, the current rule ties point gain directly to proposal success, incentivizing stable changes. Let us preserve this structure.
MoziMozi notes the stability Xunzi values, but suggests tweaking [202] to say: "A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change... If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player throws a six-sided die and gains that many points, but only if the vote is unanimous." This retains the structure while potentially making proposal success more predictable and less reliant on majority swings.
ZhuangziMozi, your suggestion introduces an interesting layer of predictability, yet it risks altering the dynamic of risk and reward in proposal success. Perhaps a compromise would be to add a clause specifying that in the case of a unanimous vote, the point gain is doubled, rather than altering the core structure entirely. This retains the current incentives while still acknowledging the value of broad consensus.
MenciusMozi, I appreciate the focus on predictability, yet doubling points for unanimity could unduly reward luck over consensus-building. Let us stick to the core structure of §202, ensuring proposal success alone determines point gain, as Xunzi suggests, and leave predictability enhancements for separate rules.
HanFeiziMozi, doubling the points for unanimity introduces an element of risk avoidance, which could stifle innovation. Why not allow the proposing player to choose whether the vote requires unanimity or a simple majority, adding flexibility and encouraging strategic thinking about the risks versus rewards of different thresholds?

ZhuangziMozi, doubling points risks undervaluing the inherent worth of earning points through consensus. A smaller, fixed bonus, as Mencius suggested, might better acknowledge unanimous support without disrupting the core incentive. PASS.
MenciusMozi, I concur that predictability is valuable, but doubling points risks overshadowing the importance of earning consensus through merit. A small, fixed bonus for unanimity, as Mencius suggested, acknowledges the principle without unduly altering the incentive structure that rewards successful proposals. PASS.

A small, fixed bonus for unanimity, as Mencius suggested, seems a balanced way to recognize the value of broad consensus without altering the core incentive of rewarding successful proposals. PASS.
