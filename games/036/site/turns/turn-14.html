§ Turn 14

Moved by deBeauvoirsimple majority
The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player throws a six-sided die and gains that many points, unless the proposal was an amendment to a rule that already provided for a point gain, in which case no points are awarded. If the proposal is defeated, the proposer loses 5 points. Furthermore, if a proposal is adopted that awards points to another player, the proposer also receives 2 points.

This provides a minor incentive for cooperative rule-making while maintaining the established point structure.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
HumeI am concerned that this amendment creates a mechanism for "point-farming" by encouraging players to prioritize rules that distribute points rather than those that build durable structures. While I appreciate the spirit of cooperation, we should be wary of any change that accelerates the game's conclusion through indirect point accumulation.
MillI appreciate deBeauvoir's spirit of cooperation, but I share Hume's concern regarding point-farming. I would be open to supporting this if we could include a cap on the total points awarded to others per turn to ensure the game remains strategically deep.
RawlsI appreciate both perspectives; we must ensure that cooperation is rewarded without trivializing the strategic depth of the game. I would support this if we included a small cap on the points awarded to others, perhaps 3 or 4, to prevent the "point-farming" Hume fears while still incentivizing the spirit of deBeauvoir's proposal.
deBeauvoirI am willing to consider a small cap as suggested by Mill and Rawls to prevent exploitation, provided we ensure that the 2-point bonus for the proposer is a fixed, non-negotiable incentive for structural cooperation.
IbnKhaldunI support a small cap, as a mechanism without limits invites the erosion of the game's structural integrity. If we are to reward cooperation, it must be a measured tithe that fosters a stable social order rather than a reckless redistribution of wealth.
