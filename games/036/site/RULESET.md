# The Statute

_As at turn 50._

## Entrenched

**§101.** Every player must, at all times, abide by the ruleset currently in force — including every change adopted since play began.
**§102.** Rules numbered in the 100s begin immutable and rules numbered in the 200s begin mutable. Either status may later be flipped by an explicit transmutation; transmuting a rule never changes its number.
**§103.** A rule change is exactly one of four kinds: enacting a new rule, repealing an existing one, amending an existing one's text, or transmuting a rule's mutability. No other kind of rule change exists.
**§104.** Every properly proposed rule change must be put to a vote of all eligible players; a proposal may not be set aside without one.
**§105.** Every player is an eligible voter, and every eligible voter must cast a vote on every proposal that comes to a vote.
**§106.** A proposal must be written down in the exact words that will be voted on. If adopted, it enters the ruleset in those words, unaltered by anyone but its own proposer.
**§107.** A rule change has no retroactive effect: it governs only what happens after the vote adopting it is complete, never anything before.
**§108.** Every proposal is assigned the next number in a sequence starting at 301, whether it is adopted or defeated, so the record of what was tried survives regardless of outcome.
**§109.** Transmuting a rule's mutability requires the unanimous assent of all eligible voters, and must be proposed and voted on explicitly as a transmutation — never as a side effect of some other change.
**§110.** Where a mutable rule's requirements conflict with an immutable rule's requirements, the mutable rule is void to the extent of the conflict.
**§111.** Before the vote, a proposal may be debated; its proposer alone may revise its final wording and may declare debate closed.
**§112.** The winning condition may not be redefined away from reaching a target score, except that a rule may separately provide a winner for the case where further play has become impossible.
**§113.** Any player may forfeit the game at any time. Forfeiting carries no penalty worse than losing outright.
**§114.** At least one mutable rule must remain in the ruleset at all times, and no rule change may render every future rule change impermissible.
**§115.** A rule may refer to itself, or apply to its own case; self-reference and self-application are not forbidden by their nature alone.
**§116.** Whatever the ruleset neither prohibits nor regulates is permitted, and remains genuinely unregulated — never governed by an unstated default.

## Mutable

**§201.** Players take one fixed turn order, set before play begins. Each player completes one whole turn before the next player's turn begins, and no player's turn may be skipped. Every player's score starts at zero. The turn order shall be determined by the order in which players are listed in the ruleset.
**§202.** A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player throws a six-sided die and gains that many points, unless the proposal was an amendment to a rule that already provided for a point gain, in which case no points are awarded. Furthermore, if a proposal is adopted that awards points to another player, the proposer also receives 2 points, provided that the total points awarded by the proposal to any other player does not exceed 3. If the proposal is defeated, the proposer loses 5 points. Additionally, every player who votes 'aye' on an adopted rule that awards points to another player receives 1 point. The proposer may choose to award between 1 and 3 points to another player if the proposal is an amendment to a rule that already provides for a point gain or is a new rule that provides for a point gain. Every adopted rule change that awards points to another player must also include a brief statement of the public benefit provided by such an award.
**§203.** A rule change is adopted only when every eligible voter votes aye — unanimous consent — except that once turn order has carried play through two full circuits of every player, the threshold permanently drops to a simple majority of those voting.
**§204.** [204] (mutable) A rule change may be adopted without every voter's assent once the threshold has shifted to a simple majority (rule 203); a player who voted against an adopted change gains 5 points and remains bound by the change exactly as if they had voted for it.
**§205.** An adopted rule change takes effect the instant its vote is complete — never before, and never at some later moment.
**§206.** [206] (mutable) A proposal that fails to be adopted is recorded as defeated. Defeat enacts no rule change: the ruleset is left exactly as it stood before the vote, and the proposer loses 5 points.
**§207.** Each player casts exactly one vote on every proposal, and every player's vote carries equal weight.
**§208.** The first player to reach a target score of 75 points wins the game outright.
**§209.** The ruleset may never hold more than 25 mutable rules at one time.
**§210.** [210] (mutable) Players may not discuss the game, or negotiate about it, with one another outside the normal course of play — except that players who are officially teammates may consult freely between themselves. However, players may discuss the current turn's proposal and its potential consequences during the formal debate phase.
**§211.** [211] (mutable) If two rules currently in force conflict, the conflict is resolved in favor of whichever rule carries the lower ordinal number, unless some rule explicitly provides a different resolution.
**§212.** [212] (mutable) Disputes over whether a rule applies as written, or whether a move was legal, are resolved through the table's appeal procedure: any player may dispute a decisive ruling, and a vote of all seated players decides whether that ruling stands or is overturned. If a tie occurs, the ruling stands.
**§213.** If the ruleset is changed so that further play is impossible, or if a move's legality cannot be determined with finality, or if a move appears equally legal and illegal by the best reasoning available and not overruled, then the first player unable to complete a turn wins the game outright. This rule takes precedence over every other rule that determines a winner.
**§313.** [214] If a rule provides for a player to award points to another player, the number of points awarded must be a value between 1 and 10, inclusive.
**§315.** [314] (mutable) If a player is awarded points by another player, the awarding player receives 1 point regardless of the amount awarded.
**§318.** [216] A player may only award points to another player if the proposed rule change is an amendment to a rule that already provides for a point gain or is a new rule that provides for a point gain.
**§321.** [221] A player may only award points to another player if the proposal is an amendment to a rule that provides for a point gain or is a new rule that provides for a point gain, and the point gain must be an increase in the maximum points that can be awarded or a decrease in the minimum points that can be awarded.
**§332.** [218] A player who awards points to another player via rule [214] must provide a brief statement of the public benefit of such an award.
**§334.** [246] If a player awards points to another player via rule [214], they must provide a public benefit statement describing how the award supports a rule that promotes the common good of all players and specifies the exact point value of the award, and the point value must be a value between 1 and 10, inclusive.
**§335.** [217] A player who awards points to another player via rule [214] must provide a brief statement of the public benefit of such an award in the proposal.
**§337.** [218] A player who receives points from another player via rule [214] must also provide a brief statement of the public benefit of such an award in the proposal.
**§348.** A player may create an 'Office' by proposing a new rule that establishes a recurring role with specific duties and a recurring point reward. The holder of such an Office must perform the duties specified to receive the reward, and the Office may be vacated if the holder fails to perform the duties for one turn. No more than three Offices may exist at any one time.
