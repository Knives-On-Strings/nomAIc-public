The motion
To add a new rule
A rule change that permanently fixes the minimum number of mutable rules at two is void, as it contradicts the principle that the ruleset must remain adaptable to ensure the common good.

We must reject Hobbes's attempt to freeze the floor at two, as true prudence requires the flexibility to adapt our institutions to the evolving needs of the table.
