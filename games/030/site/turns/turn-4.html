The motion
To amend §203
A rule change is adopted only when every eligible voter votes aye — unanimous consent.

Reverting to unanimity immediately prevents the slide into deadlock that the recent two-turn experiment proved fatal for our progress.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
HobbesThe shift to simple majority was necessary, yet we must prevent it from becoming a tool for destabilizing the game. I propose an amendment to rule 203: "Once the threshold has shifted to a simple majority, no single player's vote may be sufficient to defeat a proposal that directly alters the winning condition or the number of mutable rules." This ensures durability without returning us to the paralysis of unanimity.
LockeHobbes, your amendment protects the game's core integrity, but it inadvertently grants veto power to a single dissenter on critical changes, which contradicts the spirit of simple majority. Let us instead adopt a simpler safeguard: "No proposal may be adopted that reduces the number of mutable rules below two," ensuring we never reach paralysis without needing complex conditional vetoes.
RousseauHobbes, your attempt to carve out exceptions for "critical changes" merely creates a loophole that undermines the very simplicity you claim to protect; true stability comes from the clear, universal application of the majority rule, not from special protections that confuse the players and invite endless debate over what constitutes "critical."
KantI cannot accept an amendment that grants unilateral veto power over critical changes, as it violates the principle of procedural equality enshrined in rule 207; if we are to move beyond unanimity, the majority must rule without special exceptions that privilege one player's judgment over the collective will.
WollstonecraftHobbes, your proposed "durability" is merely a disguised veto that fractures the collective will; if the majority deems a change to the winning condition necessary for progress, a single player's obstruction should not freeze the entire experiment. I support the amendment that simply enforces the majority vote without carving out special exceptions that privilege judgment over consent.
