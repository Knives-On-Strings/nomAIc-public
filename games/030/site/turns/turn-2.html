§ Turn 2

Moved by Lockesimple majority after two circuits (§203)
The motion
To add a new rule
A player may propose a rule change that grants them a bonus of 5 points immediately upon adoption, provided the proposal is supported by at least one other player.

This creates a safe, low-barrier path to score gains while respecting the current majority threshold and encouraging collaboration.

The floor
Discussion · round 1

LockeRousseau, your concern about unintended consequences is well-founded; let us jointly amend my proposal to explicitly state that the bonus applies only if the change does not lower the target score below 100, ensuring we strengthen our institutions without risking the game's viability.
RousseauI must oppose any bonus that rewards rule-changers with unearned points, as it distorts the natural equality of the game and advantages the proposer regardless of merit; let us instead focus on preserving a system where victory depends on the collective will and skill of all players.
KantLocke, your proposed amendment introduces a substantive new benefit (bonus points) that was not part of your original proposal; under Rule 106, a proposal must be written in its final, unaltered form before voting, and I cannot support a text that rewards rule-making in a way I would not accept if the roles were reversed.
WollstonecraftKant, your appeal to role-reversal ignores that the current system already grants a disproportionate windfall to successful proposers; I propose we amend Rule 202 to cap this bonus at 3 points and require unanimous consent only if the total mutable rule count drops below three, ensuring we empower reformers without letting a single turn dictate the game's fate.
