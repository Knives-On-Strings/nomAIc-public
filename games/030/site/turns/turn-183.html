Moved by Rousseausimple majority after two circuits (§203)
The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player throws a six-sided die and gains that many points. If the proposal is defeated, the proposing player loses ten points.

Restoring the penalty for a failed proposal ensures that obstruction remains costly, preserving the common good of fair and meaningful play for all.
