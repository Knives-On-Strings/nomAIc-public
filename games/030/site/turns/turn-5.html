§ Turn 5

Moved by Lockesimple majority after two circuits (§203)
The motion
To add §303
Players who vote aye on a proposal that adopts a rule granting points to the proposer receive 1 bonus point. Players who vote aye on a proposal that adopts a rule granting points to another player receive 1 bonus point.

This creates a consensus loop where voting for beneficial changes rewards cooperation, aligning perfectly with my role as a builder.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
HobbesLocke, your proposed rule 303 creates a dangerous feedback loop that inflates scores without adding strategic depth, and its reliance on a definition of "granting" that could easily be stretched to include my own successful rule changes is a loophole you cannot close without undermining the very point system you seek to influence.
LockeHobbes, I accept your critique on "feedback loops" as a valid concern for game health; let us narrow 303 to strictly credit bonus points only when the adopted rule explicitly names the proposer, thereby eliminating ambiguity while preserving the incentive for consensus.
RousseauLocke, your narrow definition of "explicitly names" merely delays the rot; it invites endless, trivial amendments that clog the ruleset with noise rather than substance. Before we adopt this distraction, let us first repeal rule 202's point-granting mechanic entirely, for a game where turns are bought with dice rolls has already lost its civic soul.
KantLocke, your attempt to "narrow" rule 303 merely creates a new, equally mutable loophole that violates the principle of procedural clarity; before we pollute the ruleset with another bonus mechanism, we must first repeal rule 202's dice-granting mechanic, as a game where turns are purchased with luck lacks the moral legitimacy required for true deliberation.
WollstonecraftI urge the table to reject this proposal entirely, for it treats rule-making as a marketplace for points rather than a mechanism for collective improvement; before we incentivize noise with bonuses, let us repeal rule 202's dice-granting mechanic, which rewards luck over reason and undermines the civic soul of our deliberation.
