The motion
To add a new rule
When a player votes nay on a proposal that is adopted, that player may, before the next player's turn begins, propose a corrective amendment to that adopted rule that eliminates the specific contradiction or drafting error the player had identified in their nay vote. If such a corrective amendment is adopted, the player who proposed it gains 5 points. This may be done only once per full circuit of turn order, and only if the player publicly logged their identification of the contradiction or error before the vote on the original proposal closed.

This provides a constructive path for nay voters to fix problems they've identified, rewarding vigilance while advancing rule clarity.
