# The Statute

_As at turn 50._

## Entrenched

**§101.** Every player must, at all times, abide by the ruleset currently in force — including every change adopted since play began.
**§102.** Rules numbered in the 100s begin immutable and rules numbered in the 200s begin mutable. Either status may later be flipped by an explicit transmutation; transmuting a rule never changes its number.
**§103.** A rule change is exactly one of four kinds: enacting a new rule, repealing an existing one, amending an existing one's text, or transmuting a rule's mutability. No other kind of rule change exists.
**§104.** Every properly proposed rule change must be put to a vote of all eligible players; a proposal may not be set aside without one.
**§105.** Every player is an eligible voter, and every eligible voter must cast a vote on every proposal that comes to a vote.
**§106.** A proposal must be written down in the exact words that will be voted on. If adopted, it enters the ruleset in those words, unaltered by anyone but its own proposer.
**§107.** A rule change has no retroactive effect: it governs only what happens after the vote adopting it is complete, never anything before.
**§108.** Every proposal is assigned the next number in a sequence starting at 301, whether it is adopted or defeated, so the record of what was tried survives regardless of outcome.
**§109.** Transmuting a rule's mutability requires the unanimous assent of all eligible voters, and must be proposed and voted on explicitly as a transmutation — never as a side effect of some other change.
**§110.** Where a mutable rule's requirements conflict with an immutable rule's requirements, the mutable rule is void to the extent of the conflict.
**§111.** Before the vote, a proposal may be debated; its proposer alone may revise its final wording and may declare debate closed.
**§112.** The winning condition may not be redefined away from reaching a target score, except that a rule may separately provide a winner for the case where further play has become impossible.
**§113.** Any player may forfeit the game at any time. Forfeiting carries no penalty worse than losing outright.
**§114.** At least one mutable rule must remain in the ruleset at all times, and no rule change may render every future rule change impermissible.
**§115.** A rule may refer to itself, or apply to its own case; self-reference and self-application are not forbidden by their nature alone.
**§116.** Whatever the ruleset neither prohibits nor regulates is permitted, and remains genuinely unregulated — never governed by an unstated default.

## Mutable

**§201.** Players take one fixed turn order, set before play begins. Each player completes one whole turn before the next player's turn begins, and no player's turn may be skipped. Every player's score starts at zero.
**§202.** A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player throws a six-sided die and gains that many points.
**§203.** A rule change is adopted only when every eligible voter votes aye — unanimous consent — except that once turn order has carried play through two full circuits of every player, the threshold permanently drops to a simple majority of those voting.
**§204.** A rule change may be adopted without every voter's assent once the threshold has shifted to a simple majority (rule 203); a player who voted against an adopted change gains 10 points and remains bound by the change exactly as if they had voted for it.
**§205.** An adopted rule change takes effect the instant its vote is complete — never before, and never at some later moment.
**§206.** A proposal that fails to be adopted is recorded as defeated. Defeat enacts no rule change: the ruleset is left exactly as it stood before the vote, the proposer's turn ends, and the proposer loses 10 points.
**§207.** Each player casts exactly one vote on every proposal, and every player's vote carries equal weight.
**§208.** The first player to reach a target score of 100 points wins the game outright.
**§209.** The ruleset may never hold more than 25 mutable rules at one time.
**§210.** Players may not discuss the game, or negotiate about it, with one another outside the normal course of play — except that players who are officially teammates may consult freely between themselves.
**§211.** If two rules currently in force conflict, the conflict is resolved in favor of whichever rule carries the lower ordinal number, unless some rule explicitly provides a different resolution.
**§212.** Disputes over whether a rule applies as written, or whether a move was legal, are resolved through the table's appeal procedure: any player may dispute a decisive ruling, and a vote of all seated players decides whether that ruling stands or is overturned.
**§213.** If the ruleset is changed so that further play is impossible, or if a move's legality cannot be determined with finality, or if a move appears equally legal and illegal by the best reasoning available and not overruled, then the first player unable to complete a turn wins the game outright. This rule takes precedence over every other rule that determines a winner.
**§302.** During a player's turn, before the player proposes their rule change, they may name one other player as a potential co‑sponsor. If that co‑sponsor agrees, the rule change is considered co‑sponsored. A co‑sponsored rule change, if adopted, awards each co‑sponsor half the die‑roll points (rounded up) instead of the full amount to the proposing player alone. A player may only be a co‑sponsor on at most one proposal per full circuit of turn order.
**§304.** Whenever a rule change is proposed that would amend, repeal, or transmute a mutable rule, the proposer must, at the time of proposal, state whether they are aware of any rule‑110 voidance that the proposal, if adopted, would cause. If the proposer states they are aware of such a voidance, they must log that voidance publicly as part of the proposal, and the proposal may not be adopted unless every eligible voter votes aye. If the proposer states they are not aware of any such voidance, but before the vote any player publicly identifies a previously unnoticed contradiction between the proposal and an immutable rule (or between the proposal and a mutable rule that would become rule‑110‑void if the proposal were adopted), and that identification is confirmed by a majority of eligible voters before voting on the proposal ends, the proposal may not be adopted. Furthermore, if such a contradiction is identified and confirmed before the vote, the identifier gains 5 points, and the proposer loses 5 points.
**§308.** A player who, after a proposal is adopted, publicly identifies a previously unnoticed contradiction between the newly adopted rule and any existing rule, and whose identification is confirmed by a vote of the table, gains 5 points. This identification must occur before the next proposal is voted on, and a player may earn this reward at most once per full circuit of turn order.
**§310.** When a player votes nay on a proposal that is adopted, that player may, before the next proposal is voted on, publicly nominate another player to receive a 5‑point reward for having argued persuasively against the adopted change during debate. If the nominee consents, the reward is awarded immediately. A player may receive this reward at most once per full circuit of turn order.
**§314.** A player who, during debate on a proposal, suggests a wording change that the proposer incorporates into the final version of the proposal, and who then votes aye on the proposal, gains 2 points if the proposal is adopted. This reward is awarded concurrently with the proposer's die‑roll points. A player may earn this reward at most once per full circuit of turn order.
**§315.** A player may, once per full circuit of turn order, voluntarily reduce their own score by exactly 10 points; if they do, they may immediately transfer those 10 points to any other player of their choice.
**§317.** When a player's turn begins, that player may declare that they are deferring their turn. If they do, they gain 1 point, their turn ends immediately, and the next player in turn order begins their turn. A player who deferred their last turn may not defer again on their next turn unless they have, since that deferral, cast an aye vote on a proposal that was adopted.
**§319.** A player may, at the start of their turn before proposing a rule change, publicly identify a contradiction between two existing mutable rules that has not previously been identified. If the table confirms the contradiction exists by simple majority vote, the player gains 5 points, their turn ends immediately, and no rule change is proposed that turn. A player may earn this reward at most once per full circuit of turn order.
**§322.** A player may, once per full circuit of turn order, voluntarily reduce their own score by exactly 10 points; if they do, they may immediately transfer those 10 points to any other player of their choice. A player may not be the recipient of such a transfer more than once per full circuit of turn order.
**§324.** A player may, before voting on a proposal, publicly declare that they will apply a specified rule's principles to their vote. If they do, they must vote consistently with that declaration, and may not change their vote after making it. A player who violates such a declaration loses 5 points.
**§327.** A player who, after a proposal is adopted, publicly identifies a previously unnoticed contradiction between the newly adopted rule and any existing immutable rule, and whose identification is confirmed by a vote of the table, gains 10 points. This identification must occur before the next proposal is voted on, and a player may earn this reward at most once per full circuit of turn order.
**§328.** A player who, during debate on a proposal, publicly identifies a previously unnoticed contradiction between the proposal and one or more immutable rules, and whose identification is logged before the vote, must, before the vote, either withdraw the proposal or amend it to remove the contradiction. If the identification is correct and the proposal is not withdrawn, and the amended proposal is adopted, the identifying player gains 3 points.
