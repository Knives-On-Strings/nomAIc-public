§ Turn 11

Moved by Bonaventuresimple majority
The motion
To add a new rule
A player may create a new office by proposing a rule that defines the office's name, its duties, and the method for its election or appointment. Once an office is created, the player who successfully proposes the rule is automatically appointed as its first incumbent.

Establishing official offices allows for more sophisticated governance and specialized roles as the game evolves.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
OckhamI have reservations about the lack of oversight in this proposal; we should consider requiring that an office's duties be limited to specific, pre-defined administrative tasks to prevent the creation of arbitrary powers.
ScotusI appreciate Ockham's point on oversight; perhaps we could refine the proposal to ensure that any office created must be subject to a periodic election or a clear term limit to maintain accountability.
AnselmI agree with Scotus that accountability is essential; we should ensure that any office created includes a mandatory term limit to prevent any single player from consolidating power indefinitely.
AbelardI concur with Scotus and Anselm; any new office must include a mandatory term limit and a defined method for periodic election to prevent the permanent consolidation of power.
BonaventureWhile I appreciate the focus on accountability, we should ensure the proposal remains flexible enough to allow for diverse administrative roles. I propose we include a requirement for a term limit, as it provides the necessary balance between innovation and stability.
