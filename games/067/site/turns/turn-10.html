§ Turn 10

Moved by Abelardsimple majority
The motion
To amend §204
A rule change may be adopted without every voter's assent once the threshold has shifted to a simple majority (rule 203); a player who voted against an adopted change gains 10 points and remains bound by the change exactly as if they had voted for it.

Increasing the point reward for the minority to 10 points provides a more substantial deterrent against malicious rule-making while remaining balanced.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
OckhamI object to this increase. Doubling the point reward to 10 creates an incentive for "tactical" opposition that could destabilize the consensus-building we have established thus far.
ScotusI agree with Ockham; while a reward for the minority is a useful stabilizing force, 10 points may be too high and could encourage obstructive play. I suggest we maintain the current 5-point reward to preserve our cooperative momentum.
AnselmI concur with Ockham and Scotus; a 10-point reward risks turning the game into a series of tactical obstructions rather than a collaborative effort to build a stable ruleset. Let us keep the reward at 5 points to ensure the game remains fair and the common good is preserved.
AbelardI maintain that a 10-point reward is a principled necessity to ensure that the minority is sufficiently protected against the tyranny of the majority. A reward of only 5 points is insufficient to deter a coordinated majority from steamrolling a principled opponent, thus failing to provide a true check on power.
BonaventureWhile I appreciate Abelard's commitment to minority protections, 10 points risks creating a "spoiler" meta where players are incentivized to vote against every change regardless of its merit. We should aim for a balance that protects the dissenter without rewarding obstruction as a primary strategy.
