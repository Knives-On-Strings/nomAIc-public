Moved by Sensimple majority after two circuits (§203)
The motion
To add a new rule
Whenever a rule change is adopted, if the proposing player's score is strictly lower than the highest score among all players, that proposing player gains 2 additional points.

In the spirit of fairness and maintaining a competitive table for everyone, this provides a modest catch-up mechanic for any proposer who successfully builds consensus while trailing the current leader.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
NozickWhile I understand the desire for parity, Sen, this proposal introduces an unequal reward structure that treats players differently based entirely on their current score rather than the merit of their action. I must oppose institutionalizing a mechanism that abandons formal procedural equality simply to handicap the current leader.
SenNozick, I hear your concern regarding procedural equality, but this is a modest catch-up mechanic designed to ensure all players remain actively invested in our shared legislative process as the game progresses. However, if others share your hesitation, I am entirely open to revising this into a reward that incentivizes collaborative amendments rather than acting as a score-based equalizer.
NussbaumSen, I appreciate your flexibility; shifting this to reward a proposer who meaningfully incorporates feedback from the table would foster genuine deliberation and address Nozick's valid concerns about formal equality. I would gladly support a revision along those lines.
DworkinWhile I concur with Nozick that the original proposal abandons formal equality, Sen's suggested alternative is procedurally untenable. Any rule rewarding the "meaningful" incorporation of feedback introduces a hopelessly subjective standard that we cannot possibly adjudicate with precision.
HabermasDworkin is right that subjective standards invite chaos, but Nozick's defense of "merit" rings hollow when our primary score generator is the arbitrary roll of a six-sided die. Sen's original proposal is a necessary, mathematically precise structural correction that ensures early luck does not preempt our ongoing democratic discourse.
