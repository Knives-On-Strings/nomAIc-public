# The Statute

_As at turn 36._

## Entrenched

**§101.** Every player must, at all times, abide by the ruleset currently in force — including every change adopted since play began.
**§102.** Rules numbered in the 100s begin immutable and rules numbered in the 200s begin mutable. Either status may later be flipped by an explicit transmutation; transmuting a rule never changes its number.
**§103.** A rule change is exactly one of four kinds: enacting a new rule, repealing an existing one, amending an existing one's text, or transmuting a rule's mutability. No other kind of rule change exists.
**§104.** Every properly proposed rule change must be put to a vote of all eligible players; a proposal may not be set aside without one.
**§105.** Every player is an eligible voter, and every eligible voter must cast a vote on every proposal that comes to a vote.
**§106.** A proposal must be written down in the exact words that will be voted on. If adopted, it enters the ruleset in those words, unaltered by anyone but its own proposer.
**§107.** A rule change has no retroactive effect: it governs only what happens after the vote adopting it is complete, never anything before.
**§108.** Every proposal is assigned the next number in a sequence starting at 301, whether it is adopted or defeated, so the record of what was tried survives regardless of outcome.
**§109.** Transmuting a rule's mutability requires the unanimous assent of all eligible voters, and must be proposed and voted on explicitly as a transmutation — never as a side effect of some other change.
**§110.** Where a mutable rule's requirements conflict with an immutable rule's requirements, the mutable rule is void to the extent of the conflict.
**§111.** Before the vote, a proposal may be debated; its proposer alone may revise its final wording and may declare debate closed.
**§112.** The winning condition may not be redefined away from reaching a target score, except that a rule may separately provide a winner for the case where further play has become impossible.
**§113.** Any player may forfeit the game at any time. Forfeiting carries no penalty worse than losing outright.
**§114.** At least one mutable rule must remain in the ruleset at all times, and no rule change may render every future rule change impermissible.
**§115.** A rule may refer to itself, or apply to its own case; self-reference and self-application are not forbidden by their nature alone.
**§116.** Whatever the ruleset neither prohibits nor regulates is permitted, and remains genuinely unregulated — never governed by an unstated default.

## Mutable

**§201.** Players take one fixed turn order, set before play begins. Each player completes one whole turn before the next player's turn begins, and no player's turn may be skipped. Every player's score starts at zero.
**§202.** A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player throws a six-sided die and gains that many points.
**§203.** A rule change is adopted only when every eligible voter votes aye — unanimous consent — except that once turn order has carried play through two full circuits of every player, the threshold permanently drops to a simple majority of those voting.
**§204.** A rule change may be adopted without every voter's assent once the threshold has shifted to a simple majority (rule 203); a player who voted against an adopted change remains bound by the change exactly as if they had voted for it, and receives no points for dissenting.
**§205.** An adopted rule change takes effect the instant its vote is complete — never before, and never at some later moment.
**§206.** A proposal that fails to be adopted is recorded as defeated. Defeat enacts no rule change: the ruleset is left exactly as it stood before the vote, the proposer's turn ends, and the proposer loses 2 points.
**§207.** Each player casts exactly one vote on every proposal, and every player's vote carries equal weight.
**§208.** The first player to reach a target score of 100 points wins the game outright.
**§209.** The ruleset may never hold more than 25 mutable rules at one time.
**§211.** If two rules currently in force conflict, the conflict is resolved in favor of whichever rule carries the lower ordinal number, unless some rule explicitly provides a different resolution.
**§212.** Disputes over whether a rule applies as written, or whether a move was legal, are resolved through the table's appeal procedure: any player may dispute a decisive ruling, and a vote of all eligible voters decides whether that ruling stands or is overturned. No ruling may be disputed more than three turns after it was made.
**§213.** If the ruleset is changed so that further play is impossible, or if a move's legality cannot be determined with finality, or if a move appears equally legal and illegal by the best reasoning available and not overruled, then the first player unable to complete a turn wins the game outright. This rule takes precedence over every other rule that determines a winner.
**§301.** Whenever a rule change is adopted by the unanimous consent of all eligible voters, every eligible voter immediately gains 2 points.
**§302.** Whenever a rule change is adopted by the unanimous consent of all eligible voters, the player who proposed it gains an additional 2 points.
**§304.** To be considered properly proposed, any proposal to amend or repeal an existing rule must explicitly identify the targeted rule by its ordinal number.
**§306.** No player may propose a rule change that is identical in text to any proposal that was defeated within the preceding five turns.
**§309.** No player's score may fall below zero. If any rule or penalty would reduce a player's score below zero, their score is instead set exactly to zero.
**§312.** Whenever a rule change is adopted, if the proposing player's score is strictly lower than the highest score among all players, that proposing player gains 4 additional points.
**§318.** Whenever a player gains points from a die throw under rule 202, if their score before the throw was strictly greater than 25, the player with the lowest score immediately gains 2 points. If multiple players are tied for the lowest score, each tied player gains 2 points.
**§319.** To be considered properly proposed, any proposal to amend an existing rule must state the entire new text of the rule in full; proposals that specify only partial modifications or deletions of text are invalid.
**§322.** Whenever a proposal to repeal an existing mutable rule is adopted, the player who proposed it gains 2 points.
**§328.** Whenever a rule change is adopted, the player with the lowest score immediately gains 2 points. If multiple players are tied for the lowest score, each tied player gains 2 points.
**§329.** No mutable rule may be amended or repealed during the three turns immediately following the turn on which it was adopted.
**§331.** Whenever a decisive ruling is overturned through the appeal procedure established in rule 212, the player who disputed the ruling immediately gains 3 points.
**§333.** There is hereby established the office of Speaker, which shall be held by the player with the lowest score. If multiple players are tied for the lowest score, the office of Speaker is vacant.
