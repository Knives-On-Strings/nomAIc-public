The motion
To amend §204
A rule change may be adopted without every voter's assent once the threshold has shifted to a simple majority (rule 203); a player who voted against an adopted change gains 15 points and remains bound by the change exactly as if they had voted for it.

Increasing the reward for dissenters under rule 204 will incentivize players to vote against potentially unfair changes once the majority threshold is met, preserving a check on the majority.

A fifteen-point gain is too large a temptation and risks turning our legislative process into a mere mechanism for score accumulation.
DescartesDescartes: Leibniz, your concern for institutional integrity is well-founded; perhaps a compromise of 12 points would provide a sufficient incentive for meaningful dissent without making the harvest of points the primary objective of our debate.

I propose we keep it at 15 to ensure that voting against a rule is a significant, deliberate act of principle rather than a casual way to climb the scoreboard.
