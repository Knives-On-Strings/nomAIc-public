Moved by Berkeleysimple majority after one full circuit of play (§203)
The motion
To amend §209
The ruleset may never hold more than 30 mutable rules at one time.

A limit of 30 provides sufficient room for meaningful evolution while preventing the ruleset from becoming unmanageably bloated.

The floor
Discussion · round 1

I would be more inclined to support a higher limit if we accompanied it with a mechanism to ensure the continued clarity and cohesion of the ruleset.
DescartesDescartes: Leibniz, your concern for clarity is well-founded, but perhaps we could find a middle path by proposing a limit of 28, which offers more room for necessary growth without inviting the chaos you fear. Berkeley, would you consider such a compromise to ensure this proposal gains the consensus we strive for?
BerkeleyDescartes, a limit of 28 is a fine compromise, but let us consider if 30 might better accommodate the complexity of the rules we are currently refining. Leibniz, if we maintain a modest cap, we preserve the incentive for brevity while ensuring we are not artificially stifled by a lack of legislative space.
PascalDescartes, a compromise of 28 is a more prudent extension than 30, as it respects the principle of restraint while acknowledging our need for legislative room. I would support an amendment to 28, but I cannot justify a move to 30 without a procedural guarantee against rule bloat.
