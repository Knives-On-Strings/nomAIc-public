Moved by Pascalsimple majority after one full circuit of play (§203)
The motion
To amend §212
Disputes over whether a rule applies as written, or whether a move was legal, are resolved through the table's appeal procedure: any player may dispute a decisive ruling, and a vote of all seated players decides whether that ruling stands or is overturned, provided that such a vote must be unanimous.

Unanimity in dispute resolution ensures that legal interpretations are not merely products of a shifting majority, but are grounded in shared procedural certainty.
