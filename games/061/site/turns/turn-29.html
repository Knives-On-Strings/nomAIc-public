§ Turn 29

Moved by Pascalsimple majority after one full circuit of play (§203)
The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player throws a six-sided die and gains that many points, unless the proposal is to repeal or amend a rule that affects scoring, in which case the player gains no points.

To prevent players from proposing trivial scoring amendments solely to harvest points without advancing the legislative process.

The floor

If we penalize the refinement of our financial laws, we risk a stagnation where no one dares touch the most critical levers of the game.
DescartesDescartes: Pascal, Leibniz raises a profound concern; if we strip the reward from those refining our economic structure, we create a perverse incentive to leave flawed scoring rules untouched.
