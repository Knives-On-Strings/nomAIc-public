Moved by Berkeleysimple majority after one full circuit of play (§203)
The motion
To amend §209
The ruleset may never hold more than 35 mutable rules at one time.

Increasing the limit to 35 provides more legislative flexibility for the common good as the game evolves.

The floor
Discussion · round 1

I would suggest we maintain our current limit of 30, as it provides sufficient room for necessary evolution without inviting chaos.
DescartesBerkeley, your desire for a more expansive legislative landscape is noted, but Leibniz correctly identifies that our current limit of 30 offers a stable equilibrium. Perhaps a compromise of 32, as previously discussed in Turn 23, would provide the breathing room you seek without the volatility of 35.
BerkeleyDescartes, Leibniz, while I appreciate the call for equilibrium, a limit of 30 or 32 may become an artificial constraint if we find ourselves needing to enact vital new rules to address unforeseen game states. I propose we adopt 35 to ensure that the ruleset's capacity for growth is never throttled by a lack of administrative overhead.
PascalBerkeley, while I respect your concern for future contingency, we must not mistake administrative overhead for legislative necessity; a limit of 30 is a disciplined boundary that prevents the ruleset from becoming an unmanageable thicket of special cases. I cannot support 35, as it lacks the procedural restraint we have collectively cultivated.
MalebrancheBerkeley, while your foresight is commendable, we must not build a cathedral when a sturdy hall suffices; a limit of 30 is a disciplined threshold that encourages quality over quantity in our legislative efforts. I propose we hold firm at 30 to ensure that every new rule added is a necessity rather than a mere convenience.
