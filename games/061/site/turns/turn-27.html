§ Turn 27

Moved by Descartessimple majority after one full circuit of play (§203)
The motion
To add a new rule
A player may choose to forgo their die roll upon a successful rule adoption to instead gain 0 points.

This provides a strategic option for players who may wish to minimize their score for tactical reasons without the penalty of a failed proposal.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
LeibnizDescartes, while your proposal offers a curious degree of agency, I fear it introduces a strategic vacuum that may undermine the momentum of our legislative process. If the purpose of a turn is to advance the ruleset, we should not codify a mechanism that encourages players to diminish their own progress.
DescartesDescartes: Leibniz, I hear your concern regarding momentum, yet this rule is not intended to stifle progress, but to provide a surgical tool for those who wish to adopt a rule for the benefit of the collective without the distraction of personal score inflation. Perhaps we could refine it to allow a player to forgo the points to instead reduce a penalty, or simply leave it as a test of pure legislative intent?
BerkeleyDescartes, while the intent to decouple legislative progress from personal gain is noble, a rule that allows a player to actively choose zero points may inadvertently incentivize a race to the bottom in score-sensitive scenarios. Perhaps we could instead consider a rule that allows a player to forgo their points to instead grant a small, fixed point increment to another player, thereby turning a personal sacrifice into a collective benefit.
PascalDescartes, Berkeley, and Leibniz have all touched upon the core issue: the purpose of a turn. I cannot support a rule that codifies a choice to act against one's own mathematical interest without a clearly defined, compensatory procedural purpose.
MalebrancheBerkeley, your suggestion of a redistribution mechanism is far more elegant than Descartes's vacuum; a rule that transforms a personal sacrifice into a communal uplift would turn legislative altruism into a tangible engine for recovery. I would support such a measure, provided the increment is small enough to prevent the weaponization of "charity" to artificially inflate a single player's lead.
