§ Turn 2

Moved by Senunanimous consent (§203)
The motion
To amend §210
Players may freely discuss the game and negotiate with one another at any time, both during and outside the normal course of play.

Removing the restriction on communication allows us all to openly coordinate and build consensus on future proposals.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
NozickWhile I am sympathetic to the free exchange of ideas, completely abandoning this restriction risks moving the true game into the shadows where our public record cannot follow. I would prefer a more measured amendment that formalizes alliances or structured negotiation rather than a sweeping deregulation.
SenNozick raises an excellent point about preserving transparency and the public record. I will gladly revise this proposal to instead establish a formal framework for registering public teams or alliances, allowing us to communicate freely while keeping our agreements in the light.
NussbaumI appreciate Sen's willingness to adapt and Nozick's focus on transparency, as keeping our deliberations public serves the common good of the table. If we move toward formalizing teams, let us ensure the mechanics remain inclusive and do not create permanent factions that marginalize any player.
DworkinIf this proposal is to establish a formal framework for registering teams, the exact procedures for formation, membership, and dissolution must be explicitly codified in the text. I will not support an amendment that leaves these mechanics vague or reliant upon unstated defaults.
HabermasI welcome Sen's pivot toward formalizing teams; establishing a public registry for alliances not only enriches our strategic vocabulary but ensures our communicative action remains transparent. Let us draft explicit mechanics for team formation and dissolution so that every player has fair access to these new collaborative structures.
Discussion · round 2
NozickI look forward to reviewing your revised text, Sen; provided the mechanics for registering and dissolving these teams are clearly codified, I will gladly support this measured expansion of our collaborative framework.
SenI thank you all for your constructive feedback; I will now revise the proposal to explicitly codify how teams are publicly formed, joined, and dissolved, ensuring our collaborations remain fully transparent and accessible to all.
NussbaumI eagerly await the revised text, and I encourage you to include a mechanism that limits team sizes or ensures no player is permanently left isolated, preserving fairness for everyone at the table.
HabermasNussbaum's suggestion to limit team sizes is an excellent safeguard against the monopolization of discourse by a single dominant faction. I urge Sen to incorporate these limits into the final revision, ensuring our new collaborative structures empower every voice rather than stifling them.
Discussion · round 3

HabermasSen has admirably formalized our communicative structures and closed the discourse. I am fully prepared to cast my aye vote and bring this new associative framework into our public sphere.
