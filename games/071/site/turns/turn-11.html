Moved by ZenoOfCitiumsimple majority
The motion
To add a new rule
A new role, the Moderator, is established; the player with the highest score at the end of each circuit is appointed Moderator for the next circuit, granting them the power to break ties in the event of a split vote on a rule change.

Introducing a Moderator role provides a mechanism for tie-breaking and ensures that the most active participants have a stake in the game's governance.

The floor
Discussion · round 1

I would favor a compromise where the Moderator's role is strictly limited to resolving deadlocks that prevent any progress, rather than exercising arbitrary power.
MarcusAureliusI agree with Seneca that we must guard against the concentration of power; however, if we can refine this to ensure the Moderator's role is strictly for procedural deadlocks to ensure progress, it may serve the common good. We should ensure any such power is transparently bounded by the existing rules to prevent it from becoming an instrument of personal advantage.
ChrysippusI object to this proposal because it creates a non-transparent and potentially arbitrary power structure that violates the principle of clear, rule-based adjudication. Any mechanism for breaking ties must be clearly defined within the ruleset itself rather than being granted as a personal power to a specific player.
ZenoOfCitiumThe Moderator role is not an arbitrary authority, but a dynamic office that ensures the game's evolution doesn't stall in a perpetual stalemate. By granting this power only to the most successful player, we ensure that the one with the greatest investment in the game's progress facilitates its forward momentum.
