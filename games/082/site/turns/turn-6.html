§ Turn 6

Moved by DeMorgansimple majority (§203)
The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player throws a six-sided die and gains that many points. A successful proposal additionally grants bonus points equal to the number of rules currently in force.

Aligns bonus points with rule count to encourage strategic rule management.

BooleI appreciate DeMorgan's effort to introduce a dynamic element to the scoring mechanism, aligning rewards more closely with the complexity of the ruleset. However, we must consider the potential volatility this introduces. By tying bonus points to the number of rules in force, we risk escalating the ruleset beyond practical management, which could lead to unintended consequences and strategic imbalances. Instead, I propose a more measured approach: a sliding scale for bonus points that increases incrementally based on specific thresholds of rules in force. This would encourage strategic rule management without the risk of runaway growth. For instance, a base of 5 bonus points for up to 15 rules, increasing by 1 point for every additional 5 rules beyond that, up to a cap. This method maintains the incentive for strategic rule changes while ensuring the ruleset remains manageable and the game balanced. I suggest we amend [202] to reflect this approach, ensuring we foster a game environment that values strategic foresight and careful rule management. This proposal aims to strike a balance between rewarding complexity and maintaining the game's integrity and playability. PASS
