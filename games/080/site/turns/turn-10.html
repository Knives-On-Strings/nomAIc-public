§ Turn 10

Moved by Goedelsimple majority (§203)
The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted unanimously, the proposing player gains 4 points. Additionally, if a proposal is adopted with at least 80% of the votes as aye, the proposing player gains 3 points.

This amendment encourages broad consensus and rewards cooperative play.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
TarskiI appreciate Goedel's proposal for increasing the incentives tied to broader consensus. However, requiring unanimous adoption for 4 points risks stalling the game, as unanimity is a high bar that could lead to more proposals failing. I propose a compromise: "If the proposal is adopted unanimously, the proposing player gains 3 points, and if adopted with at least 80% of the votes as aye, the proposing player gains 2 points." This balances the encouragement of broad agreement without unduly penalizing players for not achieving unanimity.

This adjustment maintains the incentive for broad agreement but also encourages players to propose changes that can garner significant support, balancing reward and risk effectively.
