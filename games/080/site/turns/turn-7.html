The motion · 1 of 2
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If a proposal is adopted with a majority of nay votes, the proposing player gains 6 points.

Simplifies turn structure to focus on quality of proposals.

The floor

However, I propose returning to the original structure of one or two rule changes per turn to maintain strategic depth and variety in gameplay. Limiting it to one proposal per turn could restrict our ability to make nuanced, iterative improvements, which have been a hallmark of our current play style. Let's keep the option for two proposals, ensuring we can address complex issues more flexibly, while still rewarding skillful play with the existing point system.

This encourages thoughtful proposal-making and maintains the rewarding aspect of successful amendments, while ensuring we don't overload the voting process.

Rule 206 mandates a 10-point deduction for a defeated proposal.

The motion · 2 of 2
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player gains 2 points. Additionally, if a proposal is adopted with at least 70% of the votes as aye, the proposing player gains an additional 2 points.

Simplifies the turn structure to ensure strategic focus and avoid vote dilution.
