§ Turn 11

Moved by Tarskisimple majority (§203)
The motion
To amend §208
The first player to reach a target score of 120 points wins the game outright.

Increasing the target score extends the game, allowing for more strategic depth.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
TarskiI appreciate the discussions we've had around the target score and understand the rationale behind increasing it for a more drawn-out and strategic game. However, raising the target score to 120 may disproportionately advantage players who have already built a substantial lead, like Goedel, potentially reducing competitiveness and strategic opportunities for others. Instead, I propose a compromise: let's increase the target to 110 points and introduce a catch-up mechanism that grants bonus points to players with scores below a certain threshold, say 30 points, at the midpoint of the game. This approach encourages sustained engagement and strategy while maintaining a path for all players to remain competitive. Tarski's proposal: Increase target score to 110 and introduce a catch-up bonus.
GoedelGoedel: I appreciate Tarski's proposal to adjust the target score and introduce a catch-up mechanism, aiming to balance competitiveness and engagement. The idea of a catch-up bonus is particularly intriguing, as it aligns with our goal of ensuring that all players have viable pathways to victory. To further refine this idea, I suggest we define the catch-up bonus more clearly, perhaps specifying a bonus of 5 points for every proposal successfully adopted by a player under the 30-point threshold. This would incentivize active participation while maintaining a strategic environment. I am open to discussing the specifics of the threshold and bonus to find a consensus that benefits everyone.
ChurchChurch: I appreciate the thoughtful proposals from Tarski and Goedel to adjust the target score and introduce a catch-up mechanism. The idea of a catch-up bonus is a promising way to maintain competitiveness and strategic depth throughout the game. However, to ensure fairness and prevent potential exploitation, I suggest we add a condition that the catch-up bonus can only be applied once per player, and only if they have not adopted a rule change in the last two turns. This would encourage strategic decision-making and prevent any single player from gaining too much advantage too quickly.
KleeneKleene: I appreciate the discussions around adjusting the target score and introducing a catch-up mechanism, as they aim to balance the game and ensure sustained engagement for all players. However, to prevent any disproportionate advantages or potential exploitation, I propose that any catch-up bonus be carefully calibrated. Specifically, let's ensure the bonus is modest—perhaps 3 points per adopted proposal—and apply it only to players scoring less than 25 points, ensuring it supports those truly in need of a boost without skewing the competitive balance. Additionally, I suggest incorporating a review mechanism to assess the impact of these changes after a set number of turns, allowing us to make further adjustments if necessary. This approach maintains fairness and strategic depth while keeping the game dynamic and engaging.
PostPost: I appreciate Tarski's initiative to adjust the target score and the collaborative spirit in exploring a catch-up mechanism. While the ideas of a catch-up bonus and strategic depth are commendable, I propose a more dynamic solution: implement a scaling target score that increases by 5 points every 10 turns. This ensures the game remains challenging and engaging, preventing any player from resting on their laurels. Additionally, let's introduce a minor bonus for adopting a rule change that directly benefits all players, fostering a cooperative environment while maintaining competitiveness.

Additionally, let's incorporate Post's dynamic scaling of the target score, increasing it by 5 points every 10 turns, starting from the current target of 110. This hybrid approach combines a structured catch-up mechanism with a flexible, evolving challenge, promoting sustained engagement and strategic depth while ensuring fairness and competitive balance.

I'm open to discussing these specifics to reach a consensus that benefits all players.

This condition ensures that the bonus truly aids those struggling, while maintaining a competitive balance and encouraging strategic play throughout the game.
KleeneKleene: I appreciate Church's additional condition to ensure the catch-up bonus supports those truly in need, which aligns with the principles of fairness and strategic depth. To further refine this proposal, I suggest we also include a clause that prevents the catch-up bonus from being applied if a player has already been granted an exemption from the target score increase in the last 10 turns. This would prevent any potential exploitation of the system and ensure that the bonus remains a genuine aid for players who have consistently scored lower, thus maintaining a balanced competitive environment.
