§ Turn 15

Moved by Tarskisimple majority (§203)
The motion
To amend §208
The first player to reach a target score of 150 points wins the game outright.

Increasing the target score to encourage longer and more strategic gameplay.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
TarskiTarski: I appreciate the collaborative efforts thus far and believe increasing the target score to 150 points can encourage strategic depth and sustained engagement throughout the game. This change provides players with more opportunities to adapt and refine their strategies, promoting a more dynamic and thoughtful gameplay experience.
GoedelGoedel: I appreciate Tarski's proposal to increase the target score to 150 points, as it does indeed encourage players to engage in deeper strategic planning. To balance this extended gameplay with opportunities for recovery, I suggest adding a catch-up mechanism: each player who is at least 20 points behind the leader gains a bonus of 5 points every five turns. This would maintain competitiveness and keep the game exciting for all players, even as the target score increases.
ChurchChurch: I appreciate the intention behind increasing the target score to 150 points, as it indeed encourages more strategic depth. However, to ensure fairness and prevent prolonged play from disproportionately benefiting those with an early lead, I propose incorporating a dynamic point adjustment mechanism. Specifically, if a player falls behind by more than 30 points, they gain an incremental bonus of 2 points per turn until they are within 20 points of the leader. This adjustment would keep the game competitive and engaging for all participants, fostering a balanced and inclusive environment.
KleeneKleene: I appreciate the thoughtful proposals aimed at balancing strategic depth and fairness. To maintain the integrity of the game and prevent indefinite play, I suggest adding a rule that any player unable to make a valid proposal for three consecutive turns automatically forfeits. This ensures the game progresses and remains fair for all players.
PostPost: I appreciate the proposed adjustments for increasing the target score and the suggested mechanisms to ensure fairness and sustained competitiveness. To enhance strategic diversity and reward collaborative rule-making, I propose adding a tiered bonus system: players receive 3 points for adopting a proposal with at least 60% support, 6 points for 80% support, and 10 points for unanimous support. This system encourages players to seek broader consensus and fosters a more collaborative environment.
Discussion · round 2
TarskiTarski: I appreciate the constructive suggestions from my colleagues, especially Church's dynamic point adjustment mechanism and Post's tiered bonus system, both of which can enhance fairness and strategic depth. To integrate these ideas, I propose amending [208] to read: "The first player to reach a target score of 150 points wins the game outright. Players more than 30 points behind the leader receive a 2-point bonus per turn until within 20 points. Additionally, adopting proposals with 60%, 80%, and 100% support grants 3, 6, and 10 bonus points, respectively." This blend promotes competitiveness, collaboration, and strategic thinking.
GoedelGoedel: I appreciate Tarski's integrated proposal, which combines various ideas to enhance strategic depth and fairness. To further encourage collaborative rule-making and maintain competitiveness, I propose adding a rule that any player who contributes a proposal adopted with at least 80% support receives an additional 5-point bonus, fostering a more inclusive and strategic gameplay environment.
ChurchChurch: I appreciate Tarski's integrated proposal, particularly the dynamic point adjustment and tiered bonus system. To further ensure fairness and prevent any single player from disproportionately influencing the game, I suggest adding a rule that limits any player to proposing only two rule changes per round of five turns. This encourages thoughtful consideration and collaboration, while maintaining the balance and integrity of the game.
KleeneKleene: I appreciate the thoughtful integration of various mechanisms to promote fairness and strategic depth. However, to ensure the game does not become overly complex and remains procedurally sound, I propose adding a clarity clause that mandates every rule change be accompanied by a succinct explanation of its intent and impact. This would aid in maintaining transparency and understanding among all players, supporting informed decision-making.
PostPost: I appreciate Kleene's clarity clause suggestion, which would indeed enhance transparency. To ensure the game remains engaging and fair, I propose incorporating a mechanism where players can request a review of a rule's impact after 10 turns. If the review finds the rule detrimental to fairness or engagement, it can be adjusted or repealed with a two-thirds majority, thus maintaining strategic depth without compromising fairness. This encourages adaptive gameplay and thoughtful rule-making.
