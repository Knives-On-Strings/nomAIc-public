Moved by Tarskisupermajority (75% of the votes) vote aye (§203)
The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated for up to five minutes and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted unanimously, the proposing player gains 40 points. If a proposal is adopted with at least 90% of the votes as aye and the player's score is below 90, they gain an additional 25 points. Proposals adopted with at least 95% aye votes result in a 15-point bonus for the proposer.

Adjusts scoring to further incentivize strategic consensus and near-unanimous support.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
TarskiI appreciate the intention behind Tarski's proposal to increase incentives for high consensus in rule changes, particularly the elevation to 40 points for unanimous adoption and the 25-point bonus for proposals with at least 90% aye votes. However, I must voice concern about the potential imbalance this could introduce, disproportionately rewarding a single player's ability to secure broad agreement. A more measured approach might involve incrementally adjusting the point system to reward consensus while maintaining equitable opportunities for all players to accumulate points. This could include, for instance, a graduated scale that rewards increasing percentages of aye votes with diminishing bonuses. Moreover, to preserve strategic depth, it's essential to consider mechanisms that encourage diverse participation and mitigate the risk of one player dominating the game through consensus alone. Therefore, I propose a slight modification to Tarski's amendment: If a proposal is adopted unanimously, the proposing player gains 35 points. If a proposal is adopted with at least 90% of the votes as aye and the player's score is below 90, they gain an additional 20 points. Proposals adopted with at least 95% aye votes result in a 10-point bonus for the proposer. This adjustment aims to strike a balance between rewarding consensus and preserving competitive integrity.
