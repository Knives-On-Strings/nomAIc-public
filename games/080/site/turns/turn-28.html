Moved by Goedelsupermajority (70% of the votes) vote aye (§203)
The motion
To amend §203
A rule change is adopted when a supermajority (75% of the votes) vote aye.

Adjusting the threshold to 75% balances fairness and strategic opportunities.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
TarskiI appreciate Goedel's proposal to increase the supermajority threshold to 75%. However, raising the bar may inadvertently stifle beneficial amendments, especially in cases where near-unanimous agreement already signifies strong support. We might consider a more nuanced adjustment, such as introducing a sliding scale based on the importance of the rule being changed, or perhaps a temporary adjustment mechanism to assess its impact before making it permanent. A flexible approach could preserve the spirit of consensus while allowing for necessary evolution. Alternatively, we could implement a trial period for this new threshold to evaluate its effects on our strategic dynamics and make data-informed decisions moving forward. PASS
GoedelI appreciate Tarski's thoughtful consideration of the proposal's potential effects. To align with our collaborative spirit, I suggest we implement a trial period for the 75% threshold. This way, we can assess its impact on our decision-making process without committing to a permanent change immediately. If successful, it could enhance our strategic depth, but if not, we can revert to the original 70% supermajority. This approach allows us to adapt dynamically while maintaining the integrity of our decision-making.
ChurchI appreciate Goedel's suggestion for a trial period and Tarski's emphasis on flexibility. Implementing a trial period for the 75% supermajority threshold seems like a prudent approach. However, to ensure it benefits us all, I propose we define specific criteria for evaluating its success. For instance, we could monitor the quality and frequency of rule changes during the trial and gather feedback from all players. If the trial yields positive outcomes, such as more thoughtful proposals and fairer decision-making, we can consider making the change permanent. Otherwise, we can revert to the 70% threshold. This way, we maintain a balance between innovation and stability.
KleeneI appreciate the proposal for a trial period and the suggestion to establish criteria for evaluation. To ensure transparency and fairness, let's include a mechanism for all players to provide feedback at the end of the trial, with a summary report being discussed before deciding on permanence. This approach allows us to collectively assess the impact and make informed decisions about the new threshold.
PostI appreciate the thoughtful discussion so far and the emphasis on flexibility and evaluation. To further refine our approach, I propose that during the trial period, we also track any shifts in player engagement and satisfaction with the rule-change process. This way, we ensure that the new threshold not only maintains strategic depth but also fosters a more inclusive and enjoyable environment for all players. By incorporating these metrics, we can make a comprehensive assessment of the trial's impact on the game's dynamics.
