The motion
To amend §201
Players take one fixed turn order, set before play begins, but may request a one-time reordering of the turn sequence once per game, which requires unanimous consent from all eligible players.

Allowing a reordering can adapt the turn dynamics to emerging strategies and ensure fairness.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
TarskiI appreciate the idea of introducing a mechanism for flexibility in turn order, as it could encourage collaboration and adaptability. However, I am concerned that requiring unanimous consent for such a change could lead to strategic deadlock, especially if some players are unwilling to cooperate for their own benefit. To mitigate this, I propose a modification: instead of unanimous consent, a supermajority (75% of the votes) could authorize a one-time reordering of the turn sequence. This strikes a balance between flexibility and stability, allowing players to adapt while maintaining the integrity of the ruleset. PASS
GoedelPASS I appreciate Tarski's consideration for both flexibility and stability in turn order. The supermajority requirement seems like a thoughtful compromise to prevent strategic deadlock while allowing for occasional adaptation. This proposal could enhance strategic collaboration without risking the game's stability.
ChurchChurch: I appreciate the thoughtful approach Tarski has taken to balance flexibility with stability in the turn order. The supermajority requirement seems fair, as it prevents any single player or a small coalition from unilaterally changing the order, which could disrupt the strategic planning of others. However, I wonder if we should also consider limiting the frequency of such reordering requests to prevent potential abuse, even with a supermajority requirement. Perhaps a rule could be added to allow only one reordering request per game, ensuring that this flexibility is used judiciously. PASS
KleeneKleene: I appreciate the proposal's aim for strategic adaptability and the balance it seeks to achieve with a supermajority requirement. However, to further prevent potential strategic deadlock and ensure fairness, I suggest adding a condition that the reordering can only occur after a certain number of turns, say 10, have passed. This ensures that all players have had a fair opportunity to engage with the game under the initial order before any reordering can be considered. Additionally, I propose including a clause that any reordering must not disproportionately benefit the proposer, perhaps by ensuring that the proposer does not move to the next immediate position in the sequence. This would help maintain a level playing field and discourage strategic manipulation of the turn order. PASS
PostPost: I appreciate the thoughtful contributions from everyone. Tarski's proposal for a supermajority requirement adds a layer of balance by preventing unilateral changes to the turn order. Goedel and Church's suggestions to limit reordering requests to once per game and after a minimum number of turns further enhance fairness and prevent strategic manipulation. Kleene's idea to ensure the reordering does not disproportionately benefit the proposer is particularly insightful, as it discourages opportunistic behavior. To integrate these ideas, I propose the following amendment to Tarski's proposal: "Players may request a one-time reordering of the turn sequence once per game, which requires a supermajority (75% of the votes) and can only be requested after at least 10 turns have passed. Any reordering must ensure that the proposer does not move to the next immediate position in the sequence." This approach maintains flexibility while safeguarding against potential abuses, ensuring a fair and strategic game environment.
