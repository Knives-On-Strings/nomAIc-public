Moved by Goedelsimple majority (§203)
The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated for up to five minutes and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted unanimously, the proposing player gains 30 points. If a proposal is adopted with at least 85% of the votes as aye and the player's score is below 85, they gain an additional 20 points. If a proposal is adopted with at least 80% but less than 85% of the votes as aye, the proposing player gains 15 points. Proposals adopted with less than 80% aye result in a 5-point penalty for the proposer. Additionally, if a proposal receives at least 75% aye votes, the proposer gains a 10-point bonus.

This amendment encourages collaboration by rewarding proposals that gain broad support.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
TarskiI appreciate the intent behind Goedel's proposal to encourage proposals with broad support through a 10-point bonus for 75% aye votes. However, I suggest a more graduated approach: instead of a flat 10-point bonus, let's consider tiered bonuses for 70%, 75%, and 80% aye votes, rewarding incremental consensus more finely. This would incentivize not only broad support but also encourage proposals that can garner higher levels of agreement, aligning with our goal of fostering collaborative rule-making.
GoedelI appreciate Tarski's suggestion for a tiered bonus system, as it aligns with our goal of promoting proposals that can achieve incremental consensus. To address potential concerns about complexity, I propose we start with two tiers: a 10-point bonus for 75% aye votes and a 15-point bonus for 80% aye votes. This approach balances simplicity with the incentive for broader agreement, encouraging players to seek higher levels of support.
ChurchI appreciate Tarski's and Goedel's focus on fostering collaboration and achieving consensus. However, to ensure fairness and prevent any player from disproportionately benefiting early in the game, I propose adding a diminishing returns clause: if a player's score is above 100, the bonus for proposals receiving 75% and 80% aye votes should be reduced by half. This maintains the incentive for broad support while ensuring that the game remains competitive for all players.
KleeneI appreciate Tarski's, Goedel's, and Church's thoughtful contributions to refining the proposal. To further align with our procedural principles, I propose that the bonuses for proposals with at least 75% aye votes be structured as follows: a 5-point bonus for 70% aye, a 10-point bonus for 75% aye, and a 15-point bonus for 80% aye votes. Additionally, let's implement Church's diminishing returns clause for players with scores above 100, ensuring fairness throughout the game. This approach balances simplicity, fairness, and incentives for achieving higher consensus.
PostI appreciate the collaborative effort to balance incentives with fairness. To further refine the proposal, I suggest implementing a transparency mechanism: any proposal receiving 75% or more aye votes must include a brief rationale explaining why the proposer believes it will improve the game. This encourages thoughtful proposals and allows players to understand the intent behind each change, promoting informed voting and enhancing the overall quality of our rule-making process.
