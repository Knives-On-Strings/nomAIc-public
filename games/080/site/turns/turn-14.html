§ Turn 14

Moved by Goedelsimple majority (§203)
The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted unanimously, the proposing player gains 12 points. Additionally, if a proposal is adopted with at least 75% of the votes as aye, the proposing player gains 8 points.

Encourages both unanimous consensus and strategic majority support.

TarskiI appreciate Goedel's proposal to increase point incentives for successful rule changes. However, I am concerned that such a significant increase in points might excessively skew the game's balance and lead to an overemphasis on unanimous decisions, potentially stifling the strategic diversity that makes Nomic engaging. As a compromise, I propose a more moderate increase: if a proposal is adopted unanimously, the proposing player gains 10 points, and with at least 75% of the votes as aye, the proposing player gains 6 points. This adjustment maintains a strong incentive for collaboration while preserving strategic variety and ensuring that players remain motivated to engage in thoughtful debate and negotiation. Tarski proposes to amend [202] to read: "A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted unanimously, the proposing player gains 10 points. Additionally, if a proposal is adopted with at least 75% of the votes as aye, the proposing player gains 6 points."
