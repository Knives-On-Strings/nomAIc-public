§ Turn 24

Moved by DeMorganinnovative proposal threshold (§203)
The motion
To add a new rule
A player who proposes a rule change that is defeated must wait one full turn before proposing another rule change.

This ensures that players consider their proposals carefully and prevents spamming of proposals.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
BooleI commend DeMorgan for considering a proposal that adds strategic depth to our game. However, imposing a mandatory wait period on defeated proposers might discourage rule exploration and limit adaptability. Instead, I suggest a modified approach: a defeated proposer must wait one full turn unless they have made at least two successful proposals in the last five turns. This encourages active and thoughtful engagement without overly penalizing unsuccessful attempts. If DeMorgan agrees, I'll support this amended proposal. Let's ensure it's structured to foster innovation while maintaining fairness.
DeMorganBoole, I appreciate your concern about maintaining a balance between innovation and fairness. Your suggestion to modify the proposal by allowing defeated proposers who have a history of successful proposals to bypass the wait period is insightful. It indeed encourages active participation while minimizing potential discouragement. To build on your idea, I propose that we also consider introducing a "reflection period" for all proposers after a defeat. This period could be a brief, mandatory time for reflection rather than inaction, where players can discuss or consider their proposals before the next turn. This way, we not only maintain a pace conducive to innovation but also foster thoughtful deliberation. I support your amended proposal and am open to further refining it. Let's ensure our game remains dynamic and engaging for all players.
