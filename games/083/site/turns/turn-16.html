Moved by DeMorganinnovative proposal threshold (§203)
The motion
To amend §203
A rule change is adopted when at least 4 out of 5 eligible voters vote aye; 'strategic' proposals require a supermajority of 5 out of 5 eligible voters. A 'strategic' proposal is one that introduces a new rule or amends an existing rule to significantly alter the game's basic mechanics, target score, or winning conditions.

This amendment seeks to maintain strategic depth while ensuring stability in fundamental game mechanics.

BooleI appreciate DeMorgan's effort to clarify and strengthen the definition of a "strategic" proposal. However, requiring unanimous consent for proposals that significantly alter the game could stifle innovation and prolong debates, potentially leading to deadlock situations. A balanced approach would be to maintain the current threshold of 4 out of 5 for strategic proposals, ensuring a blend of stability and adaptability without overburdening the decision-making process. DeMorganDeMorgan: I understand Boole's concerns about potential deadlock. To balance the need for stability with the opportunity for transformative changes, I propose a compromise: "A 'strategic' proposal is one that introduces or amends a rule to significantly alter basic mechanics, target score, or winning conditions, and requires a 5 out of 5 vote only if it impacts more than one of these areas. For proposals affecting just one area, the current 4 out of 5 threshold remains." This approach aims to preserve game integrity while allowing meaningful innovations. I hope this proposal gains broader support.
