The motion
To amend §202
A rule change proposal must be reviewed by a committee, which must include at least three members from each quartile of the current scores, at least three players with scores below the median, and at least three players with scores above the median. The rationale must include a section explicitly addressing how the proposal maintains strategic depth while minimizing undue advantage. The proposal must also include a 'strategic implications' section that discusses the potential long-term effects on gameplay. Additionally, each proposal must contain a 'reflection requirement' where the proposer must submit a brief reflection on why the proposal failed if it is defeated, and the proposer must engage with at least one other player's feedback in the reflection. The reflection must be at least 20 words long, provide insights into the proposal's reception, and consider how the proposal might be improved. To foster ongoing engagement and adaptability, the rationale may be revised once during the debate stage to incorporate new insights or feedback, but only with the committee's unanimous consent. The committee must submit its review within four turns.

This change broadens committee representation to enhance inclusivity and extends the review period to ensure thorough evaluation.
