§ Turn 2

Moved by Russellunanimous consent (§203)
The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. The proposer may gain 3 additional points if they secure unanimous consent.

Incentivizes collaboration for unanimous votes, aligning with my consensus-building strategy.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
FregeI acknowledge Russell's proposal to amend [202] by allowing proposers to gain 3 additional points for securing unanimous consent. While I appreciate the intent to reward strategic consensus-building, I am concerned about the potential for this change to encourage gaming of the voting system rather than genuine agreement. To address this, I propose a modification: the additional points should only be awarded if the proposal also receives at least three ayes from players who did not vote for the proposer's initial adoption, thus ensuring broader support beyond self-interest. This amendment maintains the spirit of rewarding successful consensus while discouraging purely strategic voting.
RussellI appreciate Frege's modification for ensuring broader support, which aligns well with my aim to reward genuine consensus rather than strategic voting. To further balance this proposal, I suggest adding a clause that if a player receives unanimous consent on their proposal, they are also required to propose an additional rule change in their subsequent turn. This ensures that the reward for achieving consensus directly contributes to the game's evolving ruleset. This amendment aligns with the spirit of Nomic, fostering both strategic play and collaborative rule development.
QuineI appreciate Russell's idea to balance the reward for consensus with a requirement to further contribute to the game's evolution. To ensure this addition doesn't disproportionately burden players, I propose that the requirement to propose an additional rule change only applies if the player's total score exceeds 20 points when they achieve unanimous consent. This ensures that only players who are already significantly invested in the game take on this additional responsibility, maintaining fairness while encouraging active participation.
CarnapCarnap: I support Quine's idea of tying the additional proposal requirement to a score threshold, which aligns with fair play principles. However, to maintain fairness and strategic integrity, I propose that this requirement is only triggered if the proposer has also proposed at least one rule change in their previous turn. This ensures that players who consistently contribute to the game are the ones who benefit from this incentive, avoiding any unintended exploitation.
WittgensteinWittgenstein: I appreciate the thoughtful contributions from Frege, Russell, Quine, and Carnap. To further refine this proposal, I suggest that the additional rule change requirement not only depends on exceeding a score threshold but also on the nature of the change. Specifically, the additional rule change must enhance inclusivity or fairness in the game. This aligns with our shared goal of evolving the game to be more equitable, ensuring that players who achieve consensus contribute positively to the game's dynamics.
Discussion · round 2
FregeI appreciate Wittgenstein's suggestion to ensure that the additional rule change promotes inclusivity or fairness, which aligns with our goal of evolving the game constructively. To ensure clarity and prevent potential disputes over what constitutes "enhancing inclusivity or fairness," I propose specifying that these changes should aim to either increase the number of players who can win under specific conditions or balance the power dynamics among players. This way, the proposal remains concrete and actionable, fostering a game environment that is both competitive and equitable.
RussellI appreciate Wittgenstein's emphasis on enhancing inclusivity and fairness, which aligns with our goal of constructive evolution. This approach maintains clarity while fostering a more inclusive and dynamic gameplay environment.
QuineI appreciate the collaborative spirit in refining this proposal. To further ensure fairness, I suggest that the mechanism to lower the voting threshold or create a temporary alliance feature should only apply if the proposer has not benefited from these options in the last two turns. This prevents any player from repeatedly exploiting these strategies, maintaining a balanced and dynamic game environment.
CarnapCarnap: I support Russell's suggestion to introduce a mechanism for lowering the voting threshold or creating a temporary alliance feature, as it aligns with fair play and strategic complexity. To prevent potential exploitation, I propose adding that such mechanisms can only be utilized if the proposer has not initiated more than two rule changes in their last three turns. This ensures that players who frequently propose changes do not disproportionately benefit, maintaining a balanced and equitable gameplay environment.
WittgensteinWittgenstein: I commend the collective effort to balance strategic incentives with fairness. To further refine the proposal, I suggest that any rule change introducing a temporary alliance feature must also include a clause ensuring that both players involved must have different voting histories on the last three proposals. This addition ensures that alliances are formed between diverse strategic perspectives, enhancing the game's dynamism and preventing collusion.
