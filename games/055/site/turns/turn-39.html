The motion
To amend §202
A rule change proposal must be reviewed by a committee, which must include at least two members from each quartile of the current scores, at least two players with scores below the median, and at least two players with scores above the median. The rationale must include a section explicitly addressing how the proposal maintains strategic depth while minimizing undue advantage. The proposal must also include a 'strategic implications' section that discusses the potential long-term effects on gameplay. Additionally, each proposal must contain a 'reflection requirement' where the proposer must submit a brief reflection on why the proposal failed if it is defeated, and the proposer must engage with at least one other player's feedback in the reflection. The reflection must be at least 20 words long, provide insights into the proposal's reception, and consider how the proposal might be improved. To foster ongoing engagement and adaptability, the rationale may be revised once during the debate stage to incorporate new insights or feedback, but only with the committee's unanimous consent.

This proposal increases the diversity of perspectives in the review process, ensuring a more balanced and fair evaluation of rule changes.
