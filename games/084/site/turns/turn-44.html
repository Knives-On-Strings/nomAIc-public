The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the proposing player suggesting exactly one rule change, debating it, voting on it, and then rolling a twenty-sided die to gain points. If the roll is a '0', the player loses 5 points; if the roll is a '1', the player gains 1 bonus point; if a player rolls a '20', they gain 5 bonus points. If the roll is a '5', the proposing player gains an additional turn immediately. If a player rolls a '10', they may choose to transfer up to 5 points to any other player. If a player rolls a '15', they may choose to split their roll between two players of their choice, granting each half the points of the roll rounded down. Introduce a 'bonus round' every two turns where players can propose two rule changes in one turn to encourage innovative strategies and faster rule adoption. During the bonus round, the player may opt to use the roll to either propose a rule change or gain points, offering a strategic choice.

Adjusting the bonus round frequency to every two turns will increase strategic flexibility and rule innovation.
