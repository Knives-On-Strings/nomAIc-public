§ Turn 14

Moved by Zhuangziunanimous consent required for adoption (§203)
The motion
To amend §203
A rule change is adopted only when at least two-thirds of the eligible voters assent.

The path of perfect harmony seeks a stable yet adaptable course for our rules.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
XunziThe current requirement for two-thirds assent has led to stalemate, hindering the essential adaptation needed for the game's vitality. A majority threshold would allow necessary changes to proceed, preserving the game without sacrificing foundational stability.
MoziMozi: The current stalemate on rule changes poses a significant risk to the game's continued play. Allowing a simple majority, as Zhuangzi proposes, would facilitate necessary adaptations while still respecting the core principle of collective agreement, offering a balanced path forward.
ZhuangziXunzi and Mozi rightly point out the stagnation. Yet, a simple majority risks impulsive currents. Let us seek a middle path, perhaps a three-fifths majority, to allow the river of change to flow while still requiring substantial consent.
MenciusXunzi and Mozi, while adaptation is necessary, the two-thirds majority remains essential to prevent the rule changes from becoming arbitrary. A majority threshold would undermine the stability we have established.
HanFeiziMozi and Zhuangzi rightly seek to free the game from stagnation, yet a simple majority risks being too swift. What if we compromise to a two-thirds majority, but with the proviso that any player may request a simple majority vote if they believe the change is truly urgent and the current majority is unyielding? This balances stability with necessary adaptation.
