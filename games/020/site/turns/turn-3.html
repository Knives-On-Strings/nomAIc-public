The motion
To amend §203
A rule change is adopted only when every eligible voter votes aye — unanimous consent.

Restoring unanimity prevents tyranny by the majority and ensures every player's voice remains essential to the common good.

This preserves our efficiency while protecting against runaway changes that could undermine the game's stability.
RousseauI see Locke's instinct to protect our stability, yet his proposed safety clause is circular and unworkable: a rule requiring unanimity to pass cannot be adopted under the very majority rule it seeks to change, and a rule requiring majority cannot be adopted by unanimous consent if the majority threshold has already dropped. Let me offer a simpler path: we amend [203] to state that "A rule change is adopted only when every eligible voter votes aye," removing the entire exception about the two-circuit threshold. This restores immediate unanimity for all future votes, ensuring that no single player or fleeting majority can alter the fundamental fairness of our game, and it requires no complex, self-defeating safeguards.
