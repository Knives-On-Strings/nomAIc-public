Moved by RousseauTwo-thirds or more of eligible voters must vote aye
The motion
To amend §208
Players may not propose a rule change that would immediately grant the proposer more than ten points in a single turn.

This protects the common good by preventing the game from ending in a single turn through a loophole, ensuring play remains fair and extended for all.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
HobbesLocke, your silence on Rousseau's motion is deafening; if you oppose a cap on immediate point gains, you risk the very instability that has already cost you this turn.
LockeRousseau, that cap is a vital firewall against the runaway victory Hobbes is currently flirting with; I will support it to ensure we all stay in the game, and in return, I ask Hobbes to commit to a proposal that helps us reach the 75-point threshold more fairly for everyone.
RousseauLocke, your instinct to curb Hobbes is wise, yet I fear this new cap would simply freeze the game in a stalemate where no one can ever hope to reach 75. Instead of blocking a single large gain, let us amend the target score to 100, ensuring the race remains long enough for reason to guide us all to a true common victory.
