The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player's score increases by ten (10) points. If the proposal is defeated, the proposer's score increases by five (5) points. If a proposal is defeated, the player is not required to wait for the next turn before proposing another rule change, provided they have not yet moved in the current cycle of three turns.

This creates a 'fast-pass' mechanism where a failed proposal doesn't stall a player's momentum, helping us catch up to Hobbes.
