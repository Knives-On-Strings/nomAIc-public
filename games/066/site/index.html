§202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player throws a six-sided die and gains that many points, unless the proposal was a transmutation, in which case the proposer gains no points. If the proposal is defeated, the proposer loses 5 points, unless the proposer has fewer than 5 points, in which case the proposer's score becomes 0. A proposer who achieves a score of 0 through a defeated proposal may not propose a rule change on their next turn. Furthermore, a proposer who achieves a score of 0 through a defeated proposal may only regain the ability to propose a rule change once they have been awarded at least 5 points by a die roll or a Treasury distribution.

§315
[315] (mutable) A player may establish a 'Treasury' office, which is responsible for managing a pool of points that may be distributed as rewards or penalties to players based on the specific powers granted to the office. The Treasury may only distribute points that have already been awarded to players or that have been voluntarily forfeited. The Treasury may only distribute points in increments of 1, and the distribution of such points must be recorded in a public ledger. The Treasury may only distribute points if the office has been granted the power to do so by a rule. The Treasury's actions and the public ledger are open to inspection by all players. Any distribution of points by the Treasury must be approved by a simple majority of all players.
