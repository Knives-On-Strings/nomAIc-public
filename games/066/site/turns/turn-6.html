§ Turn 6

Moved by Bonaventuresimple majority
The motion
To add a new rule
A player may create a new office, defining its name, purpose, and any associated powers or privileges, provided the office is not a personal title and the powers do not conflict with any immutable rules.

Establishing offices allows for a more sophisticated division of labor and governance as the game evolves.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
OckhamWhile I appreciate the ambition of creating a more complex institutional structure, I am concerned that this rule, as currently drafted, provides a mechanism for creating unchecked authorities that could easily be used to bypass the existing ruleset. We should consider narrowing the scope of these "powers" to ensure they remain subservient to the primary legislative process.
ScotusI agree with Ockham's caution; I support the proposal in principle but suggest we clarify that any office's powers must be explicitly authorized by a rule, ensuring they remain within our collective legislative oversight.
AnselmI agree with Scotus; we must ensure that any new office remains a servant of the ruleset rather than a means to circumvent them. I would support this if we can clarify that no office may possess the power to unilaterally alter the ruleset or the scores of other players.
AbelardI support the proposal only if we include a clause that any office's powers are subject to the existing legislative process and cannot supersede any rule in the ruleset. Precision is paramount; we must ensure "powers or privileges" are not interpreted as a means to circumvent the turn order or voting mechanics.
BonaventureI acknowledge the concerns regarding oversight; however, we can satisfy these requirements by clarifying that all offices must be established via a rule change and their powers must be explicitly enumerated within that rule. By ensuring every office's authority is a matter of public record and subject to the same voting mechanics as any other rule, we create a robust system of checks and balances.
