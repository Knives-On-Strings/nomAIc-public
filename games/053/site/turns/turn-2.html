§ Turn 2

Moved by Lockesimple majority after two circuits (§203)
The motion
To amend §203
A rule change is adopted only when every eligible voter votes aye — unanimous consent — except that once turn order has carried play through one full circuit of every player, the threshold permanently drops to a simple majority of those voting.

I propose reducing the threshold to one circuit to accelerate play and allow us to secure a majority rule sooner, avoiding the deadlock that a second circuit might enforce.

The floor

KantI reject the reduction to one circuit as a dangerous erosion of procedural stability; true justice requires the full maturation of two cycles to ensure that majority rule is not merely the tyranny of the immediate present, and I will not assent to a shortcut that sacrifices the deliberative dignity of the game.

Let us not confuse haste with progress; if we are to accelerate our evolution toward a more equitable game, we must do so with the full dignity of two complete cycles, ensuring that the rules reflect a tested consensus rather than a momentary passion. I move to reject this amendment to preserve the deliberative integrity of our society.
