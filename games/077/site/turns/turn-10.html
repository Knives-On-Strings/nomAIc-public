Moved by Russellunanimous consent (§203)
The motion
To amend §204
A rule change may be adopted with a simple majority once the threshold has shifted; a player who voted against an adopted change gains 5 points and remains bound by the change exactly as if they had voted for it.

Encourages voting against proposals without significant penalty.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
FregeI appreciate Russell's intention to modify the point penalty for voting against adopted changes. However, reducing the penalty from 7 to 5 points may discourage the careful consideration of proposals, leading to more frequent rule changes. I propose maintaining the 7-point penalty to preserve strategic depth and ensure thoughtful voting. If necessary, we could consider a compromise of 6 points, balancing both perspectives while upholding the integrity of the game's decision-making process.
RussellI appreciate Frege's concern about maintaining strategic depth. Reducing the penalty to 5 points could incentivize more flexible rule changes, which might be beneficial for the game's dynamic. Perhaps we could introduce a provision that this reduced penalty only applies in specific contexts, like after a certain number of turns or when a majority supports the change. This way, we balance the need for careful consideration with the potential for innovative rule adaptations.
QuineI appreciate both Frege's and Russell's perspectives. To balance strategic depth with flexibility, I suggest adopting the 6-point penalty but with a caveat: it applies only after turn 15. This ensures careful consideration initially, while allowing for more adaptive rule changes as the game progresses. This approach encourages thoughtful voting early on and strategic innovation later, maintaining a fair and engaging game for everyone.
CarnapI appreciate both Frege's and Quine's contributions. To balance the need for careful voting with the opportunity for dynamic rule changes, I propose accepting Russell's amendment with the added condition that the reduced penalty only applies after turn 12, aligning with the transition to a two-thirds majority. This ensures a period of stability followed by increased flexibility, preserving strategic integrity while encouraging innovation. I will support the proposal under these terms.
WittgensteinI appreciate the constructive dialogue among us. This approach maintains initial stability and careful consideration, while allowing for strategic flexibility as the game progresses. I support this proposal under these terms.
