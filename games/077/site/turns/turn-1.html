§ Turn 1

Moved by Fregeunanimous consent (§203)
The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player gains 2 points for each side they receive in the vote, up to a maximum of 12 points.

Encourages consensus-building for greater rewards.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
FregeI appreciate the intent behind this proposal, but it deviates significantly from our current framework, which values the principle of unanimous consent. By shifting the reward mechanism to depend on the number of affirmative votes, it risks undermining this principle by encouraging divisiveness rather than consensus. Moreover, setting a fixed maximum of 12 points may create an artificial cap that doesn't reflect the true outcome of a vote. Instead, I propose an amendment that maintains the spirit of our current rules: "If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player gains 1 point for each aye vote, up to a maximum of 10 points." This change ensures that players are still incentivized to build consensus without introducing the complexity of calculating variable point gains.

This way, we can reward efforts to build broad support while still keeping the maximum gain reasonable. Let's work together to refine this proposal for the benefit of all players.
