Moved by Fregetwo-thirds majority (§203)
The motion
To amend §205
A rule change takes effect the instant its vote is complete, and affects the next proposal in line.

This ensures strategic proposals are immediately impactful.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
FregeI appreciate Frege's initiative in proposing an amendment to [205]. By suggesting that a rule change affects the next proposal in line, Frege seeks to ensure that strategic planning remains relevant throughout gameplay. However, this could potentially lead to unforeseen consequences, as the immediate implementation of rule changes could disrupt carefully laid plans, especially for those who rely on the predictability of rules to strategize. I propose a slight modification: "A rule change takes effect the instant its vote is complete, and affects proposals made in future turns." This adjustment maintains a degree of predictability and allows players to adjust their strategies for subsequent proposals, thereby preserving a sense of strategic stability. I am open to further discussion on how we can balance immediate rule enforcement with strategic foresight.

The idea of affecting future proposals, rather than the immediate one, offers a balanced approach that respects strategic foresight. This allows a brief period for players to adjust their strategies while ensuring the change does not linger indefinitely. I'm open to further adjustments if others see potential improvements to this proposal.
