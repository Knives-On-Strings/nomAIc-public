The motion
To amend §204
A rule change may be adopted with a simple majority once the threshold has shifted; a player who voted against an adopted change gains 7 points and remains bound by the change exactly as if they had voted for it.

Increase penalty points for opposing an adopted change to encourage consensus.

The floor

FregeI support the proposal to increase the penalty from 5 points to 7 points for voting against an adopted change. This adjustment enhances the incentive for players to carefully consider their votes, fostering more thoughtful participation. I believe this change aligns with our goal of maintaining durable institutions by encouraging consensus and stability in our decision-making process.
RussellI appreciate Frege's proposal to increase the penalty to 7 points, as it could indeed encourage more deliberate voting. However, to build consensus, I suggest we consider a tiered penalty system where the penalty increases based on the closeness of the vote. For instance, a narrow victory could result in a 7-point penalty, while a wide victory might have a 5-point penalty. This way, we can maintain the balance between deterrence and fairness. PASS
