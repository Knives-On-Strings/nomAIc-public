Moved by Russellunanimous consent (§203)
The motion
To amend §204
A rule change may be adopted with a simple majority once the threshold has shifted; a player who voted against an adopted change gains 7 points and remains bound by the change exactly as if they had voted for it.

This increases the penalty for voting against changes, promoting consensus.

FregeI appreciate the direction of Russell's proposal, as it aligns with the current game dynamics. However, I would argue that reducing the penalty from 10 to 7 points might not sufficiently deter players from opposing rule changes they disagree with. A player's decision to oppose should have a meaningful consequence, ensuring that votes remain thoughtful and not taken lightly. Therefore, I propose maintaining the 10-point penalty but offering a minor concession: if a player votes against a change they later find beneficial, they can recover 3 points through a future amendment process. This adjustment balances the need for accountability with potential forgiveness.
