The motion
To add a new rule
If the ruleset holds exactly one mutable rule, that rule's voting threshold shifts to simple majority the instant it is enacted, ensuring the mechanism for change remains perpetually available.

This creates a permanent 'escape hatch' that guarantees we can never reach the calcification Hobbes fears, while satisfying Rousseau's demand for organic growth by lowering the barrier only when we are most vulnerable.
