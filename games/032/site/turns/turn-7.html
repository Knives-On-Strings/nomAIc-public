The motion
To add a new rule
A proposal may be withdrawn by its proposer at any time before the vote begins, at which point the proposal is recorded as withdrawn rather than defeated, the proposer loses no points, and the proposer's turn ends immediately.

This consensus-building compromise honors Spinoza's clarity concerns while rescuing players from the 10-point penalty for failed votes that we can simply choose not to pursue.
