Moved by Spinozasimple majority of those voting (threshold dropped after Turn 10)
The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player throws a six-sided die and gains that many points. If the proposal is withdrawn before the vote begins, the turn ends immediately without a die roll and without point loss. A player whose score is negative may propose a rule change with the explicit intent of gaining points only; if such a proposal is adopted, the proposer gains 5 points instead of the die result.

I amend the core turn structure to explicitly integrate the existing rescue mechanism from Rule 377 into the primary flow of play, ensuring procedural clarity without creating a redundant or conflicting special case.
