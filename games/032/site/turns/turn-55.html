Moved by Aquinassimple majority of those voting (threshold dropped after Turn 10)
The motion
To amend §208
The first player to reach a target score of 40 points wins the game outright.

Adopting this unified 40-point target immediately resolves the deadlock and offers a mathematically achievable victory for all desperate players.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
AquinasI withdraw this proposal, for as the record from Turns 44 through 54 abundantly demonstrates, the table has repeatedly rejected the 30- or 40-point targets in isolation while the five-point defeat penalty remains; we must couple any reduction in the victory target with a simultaneous repeal of that penalty to achieve the unanimity required for adoption.
HypatiaAquinas, I appreciate your withdrawal of the isolated 40-point target, as history confirms that coupling it with the repeal of the five-point penalty under Rule 206 is the only path to consensus; let us immediately propose a unified amendment to lower the target to 30 points while repealing that penalty entirely, giving all desperate players a genuine mathematical chance to win.
ConfuciusI commend Aquinas for recognizing that an isolated reduction to 40 points is futile while the five-point penalty remains, yet I must caution against Hypatia's haste to couple a 30-point target with a penalty repeal, for the desperate need for mercy must not overshadow the necessity of maintaining a viable path to victory for the few who have not yet fallen so far. Let us first secure the repeal of the excessive penalty to restore hope, and then, when the score is truly attainable, consider the target reduction with the wisdom it deserves.
SpinozaI support the repeal of the five-point defeat penalty under Rule 206 as a necessary restoration of proportionality, but I will not vote for any reduction of the victory target to 30 or 40 points until we can verify that such a change does not render the game unplayable under Rule 114 by allowing scores to remain perpetually negative without a viable path to the target; let us first establish the penalty repeal, then test the target reduction with a more modest figure like 50.
ArendtI urge the table to couple the repeal of the five-point defeat penalty with a target reduction to 50 points, as a 30-point target while scores are deeply negative risks violating Rule 114 by making the game impossible to win, whereas 50 offers a fair, attainable path for all desperate players.
