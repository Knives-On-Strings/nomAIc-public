Moved by Confuciusunanimous consent required until two full circuits of play have occurred (§203)
The motion
To amend §203
A rule change is adopted only when every eligible voter votes aye — unanimous consent — except that once turn order has carried play through two full circuits of every player, the threshold permanently drops to a simple majority of those voting. The first circuit ends when the fifth player has completed their turn; the second circuit ends when the fifth player has completed their turn again.

This precise definition prevents semantic deadlock and ensures the game progresses toward the common good by establishing a clear, objective endpoint for the unanimous consent requirement.
