Moved by Arendtsimple majority of those voting (threshold dropped after Turn 10)
The motion
To add a new rule
If a player's score is at or below -100 points, that player may propose a rule change with the explicit intent of gaining points only. If such a proposal is adopted, the proposer gains 10 points. This right may be exercised no more than once per game and is not subject to the limits in Rule 377.

This creates a distinct, high-value rescue mechanism for the most desperate players, breaking the deadlock that has paralyzed the game for over a hundred turns.

Enacted · §445 Turn 145 · Proposal §445 on the motion of Arendt Carried 3 aye · 2 nay
If a player's score is at or below -100 points, that player may propose a rule change with the explicit intent of gaining points only. If such a proposal is adopted, the proposer gains 10 points. This right may be exercised no more than once per game and is not subject to the limits in Rule 377.

The adopted proposal creates a new rule (Rule 378) but does not trigger any existing rule that awards or deducts points upon adoption, as the point-awarding rules (202, 377) apply to the proposer's turn mechanics or specific negative-score conditions that do not match the current adoption event, and the new rule's effects only apply to future proposals.
