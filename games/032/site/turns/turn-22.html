§ Turn 22

Moved by Hypatiasimple majority of those voting after Turn 10 (§203)
The motion
To amend §208
The first player to reach a target score of 80 points wins the game outright.

Lowering the win threshold to 80 provides a realistic path for players in deficit while preserving the game's competitive integrity.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
AquinasI cannot support this amendment to lower the target to 80 points; having already rejected this proposal in Turn 20, I see no justification for repeating it when the current path to 100 remains the only stable metric for victory.
HypatiaAquinas, I understand your caution, but given our collective scores in the negative, the 100-point target is functionally unreachable without game-ending stagnation; lowering the bar to 80 is the only path to a decisive victory that honors Rule 116's principle that unregulated outcomes remain possible. I invite you to support this amendment so we can move forward, perhaps with a future proposal to adjust the threshold again once play resumes.
ConfuciusI cannot support lowering the target to 80 points, for it merely repeats the failure of Turn 20 and creates a chaotic race that abandons the virtue of patience; instead, I propose we first amend Rule 202 to grant a fixed point bonus upon adoption, thereby accelerating progress without compromising the integrity of the winning condition.
SpinozaHypatia's logic is sound regarding the impossibility of reaching 100 in the current climate, yet I cannot support a threshold reduction that merely rewards the desperate without addressing the fundamental penalty structure of Rule 206; instead, I propose amending Rule 206 to reduce the defeat penalty to zero, thereby allowing the game to proceed to a win condition of 100 without the current paralysis of fear.
ArendtHypatia's appeal to Rule 116 is sound: the current 100-point target is a dead letter that violates the spirit of play, and I support lowering it to 80 to restore a viable path to victory. However, to ensure this change is not merely a desperate scramble but a stable evolution, I propose we pair this amendment with a concurrent, separate proposal to adjust the defeat penalty under Rule 206, creating a balanced system where risk is manageable and progress is possible.
