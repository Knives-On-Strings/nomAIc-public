The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player throws a six-sided die and gains that many points. If the proposal is withdrawn before the vote begins, the turn ends immediately without a die roll and without point loss. If a proposal is defeated, the proposing player loses 1 point, unless the proposer's score is negative, in which case the proposer loses no points.

This replaces the arbitrary 5-point penalty with a uniform 1-point penalty that spares negative-score players, resolving the stalemate caused by Rule 206's repeal and Rule 377's asymmetry.
