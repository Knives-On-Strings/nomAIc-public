The motion
To add §330
A player may establish a 'Bureau' by proposing a rule that creates a permanent point-generation mechanism for a specific player, provided the rule is adopted by a simple majority.

Institutional stability is best achieved through the creation of permanent offices and predictable economic structures.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
HumeI must express my strong opposition to this proposal; creating a permanent point-generation mechanism for a specific player undermines the competitive integrity of the game and risks creating insurmountable advantages. Such a rule would fundamentally destabilize the balance of play and likely lead to a stagnant, inequitable environment.
MillI agree with Hume that a permanent point-generation mechanism would fundamentally break the game's balance. We should instead look for ways to reward long-term strategic contributions without creating runaway leads.
RawlsI concur with Hume and Mill; a rule allowing for permanent point generation for a specific player would create a "winner-take-all" dynamic that destroys the competitive balance and the common good of the game. We should seek to foster cooperation through shared rewards, not through mechanisms that grant irreversible, unchecked advantages.
deBeauvoirI concur with the consensus; a rule allowing for permanent, non-contingent point generation for a specific individual violates the principle of procedural fairness and would render the target score unattainable for others. We must maintain a ruleset where points are earned through active participation and valid rule changes, not through the creation of a perpetual "income" mechanism.
IbnKhaldunI acknowledge your concerns regarding the potential for imbalance; however, I propose that a 'Bureau' be governed by a 'Public Utility' clause, where point generation is tied to the maintenance of existing rules or the fulfillment of shared objectives. By tying these points to active contribution rather than mere existence, we can institutionalize long-term strategy while preserving the competitive integrity you all rightly defend.
