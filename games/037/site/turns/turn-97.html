§ Turn 97

Moved by Millsimple majority
The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which may be adopted by a simple majority of eligible voters.

This simplifies the turn structure to align with our current voting threshold and maintain legislative momentum.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
HumeWhile I value the pursuit of efficiency, I believe that preserving the requirement for unanimity ensures our ruleset remains a product of genuine consensus and prevents the tyranny of the majority in a game of institutional design. I would prefer to maintain the current rule to safeguard the stability of our legislative process.
MillI appreciate Hume's commitment to consensus, but I believe we can strike a balance by shifting to a simple majority while retaining our shared values of cooperation. Since we have already demonstrated a high level of mutual respect and cooperation, a simple majority would allow us to move more fluidly without sacrificing the spirit of the collective.
RawlsI appreciate the spirit of the discussion; however, I believe that maintaining the unanimous requirement for adoption is the only way to ensure that every institution we create serves the common good of all players equally. If we shift to a simple majority, we risk the erosion of the very procedural integrity that has allowed us to build such a robust and fair framework together.
deBeauvoirI agree with Rawls that a simple majority risks the erosion of our collective procedural integrity, and I maintain that any change to the adoption threshold must be subjected to the highest standard of scrutiny. I am willing to consider a majority threshold only if we can first establish a rigorous mechanism for the protection of minority rights within this new framework.
IbnKhaldunWhile I acknowledge the desire for efficiency, the preservation of the *asabiyyah*—our social cohesion—depends on the fact that no one is forced to accept a rule against their will. If we move to a simple majority, we risk the fracturing of our collective unity for the sake of mere speed.
