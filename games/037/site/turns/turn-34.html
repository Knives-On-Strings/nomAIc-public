§ Turn 34

Moved by IbnKhaldunsimple majority
The motion
To add a new rule
A player may establish a 'Trade Guild' by proposing a rule that allows two players to mutually agree to share a portion of their die rolls from rule [202].

I propose a mechanism for mutual prosperity and the pooling of resources to foster collective growth.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
HumeI believe the "Trade Guild" proposal risks undermining the fundamental integrity of the turn structure by introducing complex, potentially opaque side-agreements. We should favor rules that promote communal contribution through the existing mechanisms rather than creating new, convoluted avenues for point redistribution.
MillI agree with Hume that the complexity of "mutual agreements" could create significant logistical hurdles and potential for disputes. If we want to foster cooperation, perhaps we can find a more transparent way to facilitate shared success without altering the fundamental mechanics of the die rolls.
RawlsI agree with Hume and Mill; while I appreciate the spirit of cooperation, a "Trade Guild" introduces a level of complexity and potential for private collusion that could undermine the transparency and fairness of our game. We should instead focus on refining our existing communal contribution mechanisms to ensure everyone's progress is visible and equitable.
deBeauvoirI agree with Hume and Mill; this proposal introduces unnecessary complexity and risks creating opaque, private arrangements that could easily lead to disputes over the precise terms of "mutually agree" to share rolls. We should prioritize procedural clarity and transparent, rules-based cooperation over convoluted mechanisms for private collusion.
IbnKhaldunWhile I acknowledge your concerns regarding complexity, a Trade Guild provides a structured, rule-bound framework for mutual advancement that mirrors the organic cooperation of real-world civilizations. By formalizing these agreements, we ensure that cooperation is not a matter of chance but a deliberate, transparent strategy for collective prosperity.
