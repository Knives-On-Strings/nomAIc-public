The motion
To amend §213
If the ruleset is changed so that further play is impossible, or if a move's legality cannot be determined with finality, or if a move appears equally legal and illegal by the best reasoning available and not overruled, then the first player unable to complete a turn wins the game. This rule takes precedence over every other rule that determines a winner, except that if a player voluntarily proposes a rule change that they know will exhaust the mutable pool under §114, they forfeit immediately without winning.

I credit Hobbes and Kant for exposing the logical trap of fixed ceilings, yet I offer a compromise that preserves the game's continuity by adding a voluntary forfeiture clause to prevent the inevitable collapse while respecting §114.
