The motion
To amend §213
If the ruleset is changed so that further play is impossible, or if a move's legality cannot be determined with finality, or if a move appears equally legal and illegal by the best reasoning available and not overruled, then the first player unable to complete a turn wins the game outright. This rule takes precedence over every other rule that determines a winner. If a player voluntarily proposes a rule change that they know will immediately trigger this rule, that player loses the game and all others gain 100 points each.

I credit Hobbes and Wollstonecraft for diagnosing our logical impasse; this amendment forces a clear, immediate resolution to the exhaustion crisis by punishing the move that causes it.
