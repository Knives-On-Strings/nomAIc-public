The motion
To amend §213
If the ruleset is changed so that further play is impossible, or if a move's legality cannot be determined with finality, then the first player unable to complete a turn wins the game outright. This rule takes precedence over every other rule that determines a winner, except that if the mutable rule count reaches twenty-six, play continues with no winner declared until the count drops below twenty-six.

I credit Hobbes for identifying the cap as the moment of collapse, yet I urge the table to accept a dynamic resolution that preserves game continuity without violating §106.
