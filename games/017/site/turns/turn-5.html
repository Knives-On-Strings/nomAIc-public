The motion
To add a new rule
When a proposal is defeated, the proposer must wait one full turn before proposing a change of the same kind (add, amend, repeal, or transmute) again.

This prevents the tyranny of repetition and forces us to seek broader consensus rather than grinding the same failed ideas.
