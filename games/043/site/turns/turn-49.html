§ Turn 49

Moved by Pascalsimple majority after one full circuit of play (§203)
The motion
To amend §201
Players take one fixed turn order, set before play begins. Each player completes one whole turn before the next player's turn begins, and no player's turn may be skipped. Every player's score starts at zero. At the completion of each full circuit of play, the turn order is reset to follow the descending order of players' scores at that moment, with the player in last place moving first.

To ensure the turn order reflects the current state of play and provides a procedural mechanism for movement without the instability of constant amendment.

A fixed order ensures that every player, regardless of their current standing, can plan their strategy with mathematical certainty, whereas a resetting order introduces a chaotic element that may disproportionately benefit those already struggling at the expense of those who have worked to build a lead.
