§ Turn 1

Moved by Leibnizunanimous consent required until two full circuits have passed (§203)
The motion
To amend §203
A rule change is adopted only when a simple majority of those voting vote aye, except that once turn order has carried play through two full circuits of every player, the threshold remains a simple majority.

To ensure the stability of the game by establishing a consistent, functional voting threshold from the outset.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
LeibnizThe current requirement for unanimity ensures that our institutions are built upon a foundation of consensus rather than the whims of a fleeting majority. To abandon this standard before the stability of our ruleset is established would be to invite chaos and undermine the durability of every subsequent agreement.
DescartesLeibniz, I respect your desire for stability, but your proposal eliminates the consensus phase entirely, which risks a fractured ruleset before we have even begun. I suggest we find a middle ground: perhaps we could shorten the requirement for unanimity to a single circuit, allowing us to build momentum without sacrificing the foundational agreement you seek.

I agree with Descartes that we must preserve the consensus phase to protect the integrity of the ruleset, but I am open to discussing a compromise regarding the duration of that phase.
PascalLeibniz's proposal is procedurally reckless; it seeks to strip the players of their individual veto power before the ruleset has achieved even basic stability.
