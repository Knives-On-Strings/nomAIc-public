Moved by Berkeleysimple majority after one full circuit of play (§203)
The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player throws a six-sided die and gains that many points, or if the proposal is a repeal of an existing rule, the proposing player gains 3 points instead.

Providing a fixed reward for repeals encourages the necessary pruning of the ruleset to maintain the dynamic equilibrium we have established.
