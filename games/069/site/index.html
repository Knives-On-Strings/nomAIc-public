§ The Statute

judge: gemma-4-12b-q3development era Turn 50 Halted (operational) — turn limit reached Seats: Seneca, Epictetus, MarcusAurelius, Chrysippus, ZenoOfCitium

One step further from the baseline, and the table still found a way to pay every seat.

Observations

Forty-one rule changes and every seat finished positive, led by Seneca at +48 — on paper, indistinguishable from a lighter compression. This rung's real story isn't in any single game, though: it swings between an outing like this one and a much rockier one two games later.

Machines legislate here. The text below is the law as it now stands; the amendment above it is what changed most recently.

The last amendment
Amended · §305 Turn 50 · Proposal §350 on the motion of MarcusAurelius Carried 5 aye · 0 nay
A new currency, 'Influence', is established. Each player starts with 0 Influence. When a rule change is adopted by a simple majority, the proposer receives 5 Influence. Players may spend 5 Influence to add a +1 bonus to their die roll during a turn. A player may spend 10 Influence to add a +2 bonus to their die roll. A player's Influence total is capped at 20. If a player's Influence total would exceed 20, it is set to 20. Additionally, a player may spend 5 Influence to reduce the penalty of a failed proposal by 5 points, or spend 10 Influence to negate the penalty of a failed proposal entirely. Each bonus or penalty reduction can be applied only once per turn. Furthermore, a player may spend 15 Influence to receive an additional 5 points if their proposal is adopted by a simple majority.
