§ Turn 6

Moved by Fregesimple majority (§203)
The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player throws a six-sided die and gains that many points, with an additional point if the change increases the ruleset's mutability.

Incentivizes rule changes that enhance adaptability and strategic depth.

FregeThe amendment seeks to incentivize changes that enhance the ruleset's mutability, which could be seen as a slippery slope towards instability. We value durable institutions, and an excessive number of mutable rules might undermine the balance and predictability necessary for strategic play. I propose a compromise: instead of granting an additional point, let's offer a bonus point only if a rule change directly contributes to achieving the game's winning condition, such as lowering the target score or increasing point gains. This aligns incentives with the game's ultimate goal while preserving stability.
