Moved by Russellsimple majority (§203)
The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player throws a six-sided die and gains that many points. Players earn a bonus point if their proposal includes a structured mediation mechanism for resolving rule ambiguities and disputes, explicitly outlines how it contributes to durable institutions, or includes a method for periodic review by an independent committee. Proposals that explicitly address and resolve previous conflicts or disputes in rule application receive a bonus point. Each player also gains a bonus point if the proposal includes mechanisms for transparent, accountable governance that can adapt to unforeseen challenges. Additionally, proposals that demonstrate a clear understanding of the current ruleset and propose changes that align with the principles of durability, transparency, and adaptability will receive a bonus point. To promote durable institutions, players earn a bonus point if their proposal includes a review by a rotating committee every 10 turns to assess the impact of rule changes on the game's stability. An additional bonus point is awarded if the proposal explicitly references and integrates feedback from the last three proposals. Moreover, any proposal must ensure compatibility with existing rules, promoting stability and coherence across rule changes. Proposals must also include a clause allowing for the simplification of rule language to enhance clarity and accessibility. Moreover, proposals must include a clear plan for evaluating the long-term impact of changes, ensuring they are beneficial and sustainable. Proposals must not exceed 500 words to ensure clarity and conciseness. The proposer must ensure that the proposal does not repeat any defeated proposal verbatim within the last three turns. To foster constructive collaboration, proposals must include a collaborative clause, encouraging players to engage in pre-debate discussions aimed at refining and enhancing proposals collaboratively, with a bonus point awarded for demonstrated synergy in proposal development.

This amendment fosters collaborative proposal development and ensures alignment with game principles.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
FregeI commend Russell for proposing enhancements to our rule-making process. However, I must raise concerns about the addition of a "collaborative clause." While collaboration is valuable, mandating it risks stifling independent innovation and could lead to groupthink, undermining the diversity of strategic approaches vital to a dynamic game like ours. Furthermore, awarding a bonus point for collaborative synergy might inadvertently favor proposals that are less about strategic foresight and more about consensus-building. I suggest we refine this clause to encourage voluntary collaboration without penalizing those who prefer to innovate independently. This adjustment would better align with our shared goal of promoting a balanced, strategic, and adaptable ruleset.
RussellI appreciate Frege's perspective on collaboration and the importance of maintaining diverse strategic approaches. To address his concerns while still encouraging teamwork, I propose a slight modification: the collaborative clause could be rephrased to offer an optional pathway for players who wish to engage in pre-debate discussions, without making it a mandatory requirement. This way, players can choose to collaborate if they find it beneficial, and the bonus point can be awarded only when such collaboration leads to a proposal that is demonstrably improved by the collective input. This approach maintains the spirit of innovation and strategic diversity while promoting cooperative enhancement where it naturally arises.
QuineI appreciate the constructive dialogue between Frege and Russell. To build on this, I suggest further refining the collaborative clause to emphasize quality over quantity in collaboration. Specifically, the bonus point could be awarded when the collaborative effort results in a proposal that not only improves upon previous iterations but also introduces a novel strategic element that enhances the game's fairness and adaptability. This refinement ensures that the incentive aligns with our shared goal of fostering innovative and balanced rule-making.
CarnapCarnap: I appreciate both Frege's and Quine's contributions to refining the collaborative clause. To ensure this proposal aligns with procedural principles, I propose adding a stipulation that any collaborative proposal must clearly document the collaborative process and demonstrate how it enhances the proposal's strategic value. This transparency will ensure that the bonus point is awarded based on merit rather than mere participation, maintaining the integrity of our rule-making process.
WittgensteinWittgenstein: I appreciate Carnap's emphasis on transparency and Quine's focus on strategic innovation in the collaborative clause. To further align with our principles, I propose adding a requirement that any collaborative proposal must also include an independent review by a rotating committee to assess its strategic merit and alignment with the game's core values. This ensures that collaborative efforts are not only transparent but also rigorously evaluated for their contribution to the game's evolution, maintaining a balance between innovation and fairness.
