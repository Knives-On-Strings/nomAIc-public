Moved by Fregesimple majority (§203)
The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player throws a six-sided die and gains that many points, with an additional point if the change increases the ruleset's mutability or decreases the number of immutable rules. Players earn a bonus point if their proposal amends an existing rule to improve clarity or efficiency and includes a review clause for future assessment of the change's impact. Proposals including a mechanism for periodic assessment and potential adjustment of existing rules receive a bonus point. Proposals that reduce the number of mutable rules by at least one receive a two-point bonus. Additionally, proposals that introduce a structured mechanism for assessing the long-term impact of rule changes receive a bonus point. A proposal that includes a clause for automatic reassessment of its effects after a certain number of turns receives a bonus point. Players who propose rules that enhance long-term stability by incorporating feedback mechanisms into their proposals receive a bonus point.

Encourages rule proposals that incorporate feedback mechanisms for long-term stability.
