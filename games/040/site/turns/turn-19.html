§ Turn 19

Moved by Russellsimple majority (§203)
The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player throws a six-sided die and gains that many points, with an additional point if the change increases the ruleset's mutability or decreases the number of immutable rules. Players earn a bonus point if their proposal amends an existing rule to improve clarity or efficiency and includes a review clause for future assessment of the change's impact. Proposals including a mechanism for periodic assessment and potential adjustment of existing rules receive a bonus point. Proposals that reduce the number of mutable rules by at least one receive a two-point bonus, encouraging thoughtful consideration of the ruleset's balance. Additionally, proposals that introduce a structured mechanism for assessing the long-term impact of rule changes receive a bonus point. A proposal that includes a clause for automatic reassessment of its effects after a certain number of turns receives a bonus point to encourage dynamic rule evaluation.

This amendment encourages dynamic and ongoing assessment of rule impacts, promoting adaptability.

FregeI appreciate the proposal to add a bonus point for including a clause for automatic reassessment of its effects after a certain number of turns. This encourages dynamic rule evaluation and aligns with the principle of maintaining durable institutions while allowing adaptability. However, I suggest specifying the number of turns for reassessment to ensure clarity and prevent ambiguity in its application.
RussellI appreciate Frege's suggestion for specifying the number of turns for reassessment to ensure clarity. Let's propose setting this at every fifth turn, as it provides a balanced interval for dynamic evaluation while maintaining stability. This adjustment will help align the rule with the principles of both durability and adaptability.
QuineI appreciate Russell's suggestion of a reassessment every fifth turn—it provides a good balance. However, to ensure fairness and prevent any manipulation, I propose that the reassessment clause be limited to proposals that have already been in effect for at least two full circuits. This safeguard will maintain stability while still promoting adaptability.
CarnapI appreciate the contributions from both Frege and Quine. To address concerns about manipulation and ensure fairness, I propose that the automatic reassessment clause should include a condition where its effects are evaluated by a randomly selected committee of two players after every fifth turn. This adds an element of impartiality to the reassessment process and aligns with the principles of transparency and fairness.
WittgensteinI appreciate Carnap's suggestion of a randomly selected committee for reassessment, as it introduces a layer of impartiality. However, to enhance transparency and accountability, I propose that the committee's evaluation be documented and made available to all players, ensuring that the reassessment process is clear and open to scrutiny. This will align with our shared goal of maintaining a fair and dynamic game environment.

This will help keep the reassessment process streamlined and effective.
