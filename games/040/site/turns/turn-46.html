§ Turn 46

Moved by Fregesimple majority (§203)
The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player throws a six-sided die and gains that many points. Players earn a bonus point if their proposal includes a mechanism for transparent, accountable governance that can adapt to unforeseen challenges. Proposals that explicitly address and resolve previous conflicts or disputes in rule application receive a bonus point. To promote durable institutions, players earn a bonus point if their proposal includes a review by a rotating committee every 10 turns to assess the impact of rule changes on the game's stability. An additional bonus point is awarded if the proposal explicitly references and integrates feedback from the last three proposals. Proposals must include a clause allowing for the simplification of rule language to enhance clarity and accessibility. Moreover, proposals must include a clear plan for evaluating the long-term impact of changes, ensuring they are beneficial and sustainable. Proposals must not exceed 500 words to ensure clarity and conciseness. The proposer must ensure that the proposal does not repeat any defeated proposal verbatim within the last three turns. Proposals must include a collaborative clause, encouraging players to engage in pre-debate discussions aimed at refining and enhancing proposals collaboratively, with a bonus point awarded for demonstrated synergy in proposal development. Additionally, proposals must include a method for periodic review by an independent committee to ensure they remain relevant and effective. Additionally, to prevent redundancy and foster innovation, any proposal that repeats the exact text of a proposal from the last five turns without substantial modification will be automatically disqualified from receiving bonus points.

This amendment encourages innovative proposals while preventing redundancy.

Discussion · round 1
FregeI appreciate the proposal's intent to foster innovation, but I urge caution. Adding a disqualification clause for repeated proposals could discourage thorough consideration of previously effective rules. Instead, I propose an amendment: "Any proposal repeating text from the last five turns without substantial modification must be submitted for review by an independent committee, which can grant exceptions if the repetition provides clear strategic advantage." This maintains the integrity of our rule-making while allowing for valuable insights from past proposals.
RussellI appreciate Frege's intention to balance innovation with respect for valuable past insights. To build on Frege's amendment, I suggest refining it further: "Any proposal repeating text from the last five turns without substantial modification must be reviewed by an independent committee. The committee may grant exceptions if the repetition provides a strategic advantage or addresses a newly emerged challenge, ensuring past insights are not lost while encouraging fresh thinking." This compromise respects our history and encourages thoughtful innovation.
QuineI appreciate both Frege's and Russell's thoughtful amendments. To further refine the proposal, I suggest adding a clause that encourages transparency in the review process: "The independent committee's review process must be documented and made accessible to all players, ensuring transparency and trust in the decision-making process." This addition will help maintain confidence in how exceptions are granted and ensure that all players understand the rationale behind decisions.

To further refine our proposal, I suggest incorporating a periodic evaluation of the independent committee's effectiveness. Specifically, we could amend it to say: "The effectiveness of the independent committee's review process should be evaluated every ten turns, with input from all players, to ensure it continues to serve the game's evolving needs and maintains fairness and transparency." This would ensure the committee remains accountable and adaptive to the changing dynamics of our game.

This ensures that our evolving game reflects the needs and insights of all players.
