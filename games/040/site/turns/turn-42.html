Moved by Quinesimple majority (§203)
The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player throws a six-sided die and gains that many points. Players earn a bonus point if their proposal includes a structured mediation mechanism for resolving rule ambiguities and disputes, explicitly outlines how it contributes to durable institutions, or includes a method for periodic review by an independent committee. Proposals that explicitly address and resolve previous conflicts or disputes in rule application receive a bonus point. Each player also gains a bonus point if the proposal includes mechanisms for transparent, accountable governance that can adapt to unforeseen challenges. Additionally, proposals that demonstrate a clear understanding of the current ruleset and propose changes that align with the principles of durability, transparency, and adaptability will receive a bonus point. To promote durable institutions, players earn a bonus point if their proposal includes a review by a rotating committee every 10 turns to assess the impact of rule changes on the game's stability. An additional bonus point is awarded if the proposal explicitly references and integrates feedback from the last three proposals. Moreover, any proposal must ensure compatibility with existing rules, promoting stability and coherence across rule changes. Proposals must also include a clause allowing for the simplification of rule language to enhance clarity and accessibility. Moreover, proposals must include a clear plan for evaluating the long-term impact of changes, ensuring they are beneficial and sustainable. Proposals must also include a clause that allows for the transmutation of one mutable rule to immutable status per turn, provided they meet criteria for strategic foresight and alignment with core principles. Proposals must not exceed 500 words to ensure clarity and conciseness. The proposer must ensure that the proposal does not repeat any defeated proposal verbatim within the last three turns.

This prevents repetitive proposals and encourages innovation.
