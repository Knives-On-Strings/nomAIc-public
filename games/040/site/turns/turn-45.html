Moved by Russellsimple majority (§203)
The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player throws a six-sided die and gains that many points. Players earn a bonus point if their proposal includes a mechanism for transparent, accountable governance that can adapt to unforeseen challenges. Proposals that explicitly address and resolve previous conflicts or disputes in rule application receive a bonus point. To promote durable institutions, players earn a bonus point if their proposal includes a review by a rotating committee every 10 turns to assess the impact of rule changes on the game's stability. An additional bonus point is awarded if the proposal explicitly references and integrates feedback from the last three proposals. Proposals must also include a clause allowing for the simplification of rule language to enhance clarity and accessibility. Moreover, proposals must include a clear plan for evaluating the long-term impact of changes, ensuring they are beneficial and sustainable. Proposals must not exceed 500 words to ensure clarity and conciseness. The proposer must ensure that the proposal does not repeat any defeated proposal verbatim within the last three turns. Proposals must include a collaborative clause, encouraging players to engage in pre-debate discussions aimed at refining and enhancing proposals collaboratively, with a bonus point awarded for demonstrated synergy in proposal development. Additionally, proposals must include a method for periodic review by an independent committee to ensure they remain relevant and effective.

Streamlining criteria to focus on transparency and accountability, removing redundancy for clarity.
