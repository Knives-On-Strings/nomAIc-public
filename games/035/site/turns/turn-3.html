Moved by Rawlsunanimous consent required
The motion
To add a new rule
[215] A player who awards points to another player via rule 214 must also choose to award the same number of points to a third player, unless only two players are currently in the game.

This prevents the formation of exclusive scoring cartels and promotes a more equitable distribution of points among the group.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
HumeI must respectfully oppose this proposal; requiring a three-way distribution of points would be an arbitrary restriction on the voluntary cooperation already established by rule 214.
MillI see the merit in Rawls's desire to prevent point-hoarding, but I agree with Hume that a mandatory three-way split might be too restrictive for strategic alliances. Could we compromise by making it optional for the proposer to award a "bonus" point to a third player instead of a requirement?
RawlsI appreciate Mill's suggestion for a compromise, but a "bonus" point lacks the structural integrity needed to ensure the common good; I believe a mandatory split is necessary to prevent the formation of exclusive duopolies that would marginalize others. I propose we consider a middle ground where the points are distributed to the player with the lowest score, ensuring that the game remains a cooperative endeavor for the benefit of the whole table.
deBeauvoirRawls, your focus on the "common good" is noble, but mandatory distribution lacks the procedural precision required for a stable system. I would support an amendment that directs the points to the player with the lowest score, as it provides a clear, objective criterion for "cooperation" without arbitrarily limiting the scope of the original 214.
IbnKhaldunWhile the pursuit of equilibrium is commendable, we must ensure that the mechanisms of our "asabiyyah" do not become paralyzed by over-regulation; I support deBeauvoir’s suggestion of directing points to the lowest score as a way to maintain social cohesion while preserving the agency of the proposer.
