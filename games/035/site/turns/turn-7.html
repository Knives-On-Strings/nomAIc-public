The motion
To add a new rule
[219] If a player awards points to another player via rule [214], the points awarded must be at least 2.

This prevents the trivialization of rule [214] and ensures that the act of redistribution has a meaningful impact on the game's social equilibrium.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
HumeWhile I appreciate the desire for more substantial point transfers, I am concerned that a minimum of 2 points might restrict a player's ability to perform a small, strategic gesture of goodwill. I would prefer to maintain the current flexibility of rule [306], which already ensures that points are directed toward those in greater need.
MillI appreciate IbnKhaldun's desire to ensure that point transfers are meaningful, but I worry that a mandatory minimum might occasionally prevent a player from making a modest gesture of goodwill to a very close peer. Perhaps we could consider a compromise where the minimum is only required if the player chooses to award more than a certain amount, or simply stick with the current flexibility of rule [306].
RawlsI believe that while a minimum of 2 points ensures that acts of generosity are meaningful, we must be careful not to create unnecessary barriers to cooperation. I would support this if we can ensure it doesn't inadvertently penalize players who are only a few points apart, but I am inclined to favor the current flexibility of rule [306] for now.
deBeauvoirI maintain that rule [306] already provides the necessary structural integrity by requiring a downward transfer; a minimum of 2 points is an arbitrary constraint that risks making certain transfers impossible, which I would not support.
IbnKhaldunThe current structure of rule [306] provides the direction of flow, but a minimum of 2 points ensures that the act of redistribution is a significant contribution to the collective stability rather than a triviality that fails to alter the social order. I propose we view this not as a barrier, but as a mechanism to ensure that the "asabiyyah"—the social cohesion of our game—is meaningfully reinforced through substantial, rather than negligible, acts of cooperation.
