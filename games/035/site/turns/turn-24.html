§ Turn 24

Moved by IbnKhaldunsimple majority
The motion
To add a new rule
[234] If a player awards points to another player via rule [214], and the award is at least 1 point, the proposer may choose to receive 1 point as a 'service fee' for the act of redistribution, provided the total points awarded to the other player remains at least 1.

To encourage the virtuous cycle of redistribution by ensuring the agent of social stability is not impoverished by their own altruism.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
HumeI have concerns regarding the introduction of a "service fee" as it risks creating a system of rent-seeking that could undermine our progress toward equitable redistribution. We should consider if such a rule serves the common good or merely incentivizes the accumulation of points through the act of giving.
MillI believe IbnKhaldun's proposal offers a fair way to acknowledge the proposer's initiative in maintaining the social contract, but we might consider a "service fee" that is only granted if the total points awarded to the other player exceed a certain threshold to ensure it doesn't become a primary method of point accumulation.
RawlsI appreciate the table's focus on the integrity of our redistributive mechanisms; while a "service fee" could be seen as a minor incentive for altruism, I worry it might subtly shift the focus from the common good to a form of transaction. Perhaps we should ensure that any such fee is strictly capped or only applies when the award to the recipient is significant, ensuring the primary beneficiary remains the one in need.
deBeauvoirI find the concept of a "service fee" fundamentally problematic as it introduces a transactionary element into a mechanism designed for redistribution. If we must consider it, it should be strictly limited to a non-point benefit or a very small, fixed amount to prevent it from becoming a mechanism for rent-seeking.
IbnKhaldunI propose that the service fee be strictly capped at 1 point, as this acknowledges the proposer's role in maintaining the social order without permitting the rent-seeking that my colleagues rightfully fear. This ensures that the primary objective of redistribution remains the priority while providing a modest incentive for those who actively facilitate the common good.
Discussion · round 2

MillI believe IbnKhaldun's 1-point cap, combined with Hume's suggestion that the fee cannot be the sole point gained, provides a sophisticated synthesis that balances individual initiative with the common good. I am happy to support this refined wording as it prevents the rule from becoming a primary mechanism for point accumulation.
RawlsI believe the synthesis of a 1-point cap, combined with the requirement that the fee cannot be the sole point gained by the proposer, provides a robust framework that preserves the spirit of redistribution while acknowledging the effort of the distributor. I am prepared to support this refined wording as it ensures the primary beneficiary remains the one in need.
deBeauvoirI find the consensus on a 1-point cap and the exclusion of the fee as the sole point gain to be a necessary compromise, provided we explicitly state that the total award to the recipient must still meet the minimum requirements of rule [323].
IbnKhaldunI believe the synthesis of a one-point cap, the exclusion of the fee as the sole point gain, and the explicit requirement to satisfy rule [323] provides the most balanced path forward. These safeguards ensure that redistribution remains the primary social good while acknowledging the structural necessity of facilitating such acts.
