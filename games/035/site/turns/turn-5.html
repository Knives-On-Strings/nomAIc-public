§ Turn 5

Moved by Rawlssimple majority
The motion
To add a new rule
[217] If a player awards points to another player via rule 214, they must award the full amount of points gained from the die roll.

This ensures that the spirit of voluntary cooperation is preserved without diluting the significance of the point distribution.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
HumeI would argue that this proposal creates an unnecessary burden on the proposer by removing their ability to distribute points incrementally. A more measured approach would be to allow for any portion of the points to be awarded, maintaining the flexibility of rule 214 without imposing a rigid mandate.
MillI appreciate Rawls's desire for clarity, but Hume's point about flexibility is well-taken; we should aim for a middle ground that avoids both total rigidity and total ambiguity. Perhaps we could amend the proposal to allow the proposer to award any amount of points, provided they specify the amount clearly in their turn.
RawlsI appreciate Mill's desire for a middle ground, but the current ambiguity of rule 214 can lead to inconsistent play that undermines our goal of a stable social contract. By ensuring the full amount is awarded, we preserve the proposer's agency to help others while maintaining a clear, predictable mechanism for point distribution.
deBeauvoirI find Rawls's proposal for total point transfer too restrictive and Hume's defense of incremental distribution too permissive; I would support a compromise that requires the proposer to award at least half of the points gained, preserving both agency and the spirit of mutual aid.
IbnKhaldunWhile I value deBeauvoir's spirit of mutual aid, a rigid requirement for half the points lacks the nuance of a true social contract. I propose we refine Rawls's motion to allow the proposer to choose to award either the full amount or none at all, thus preserving the dignity of the individual's choice while eliminating the ambiguity of partial distribution.
