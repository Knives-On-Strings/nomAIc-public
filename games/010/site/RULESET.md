# The Statute

_As at turn 12._

## Entrenched

**§101.** Every player must always abide by the current ruleset as amended.
**§102.** A rule change is any addition, amendment, repeal, or reclassification of a rule. Proposing rule changes is the primary move of the game.
**§103.** If the ruleset ever becomes self-contradictory or requires an impossible action, play pauses and the situation is resolved by a vote of all players before the game continues.
**§104.** Immutable rules take precedence over mutable rules. An immutable rule may be reclassified as mutable only by a rule change that itself passes.
**§105.** A proposal is voted on exactly as its proposer wrote it. If adopted, its text enters the ruleset verbatim; no one may reword another player's proposal.
**§106.** A rule change takes effect only when adopted, and never applies to events that happened before its adoption.
**§201.** Players take turns in a fixed order. A turn consists of proposing one rule change and having every player vote on it.
**§202.** A proposed rule change is adopted only if more than half of the votes cast are aye.

## Mutable

**§203.** When a player's proposal is adopted, that player gains 1 point.
**§204.** The first player to reach 10 points wins the game.
**§205.** Every eligible player casts one vote on each proposal, and every vote carries equal weight.
**§206.** An abstention is not an objection: a proposal needing unanimous consent is adopted when no player votes nay, even if some abstain.
**§207.** When a proposal is defeated or ruled out of order, the proposer's turn ends and play passes to the next player in order.
**§208.** Points are gained or lost only as a rule provides. No player or official may grant, deduct, or transfer points except under a rule in force.
**§209.** A single proposal may enact exactly one rule change: it may not bundle several additions, amendments, repeals, or reclassifications together.
**§210.** Players may negotiate, promise, and trade support openly on the floor. Such commitments are not enforceable: only rules bind players.
**§211.** A proposed rule change is adopted if more than half of the votes cast are aye.
