§ Turn 3

Moved by Quineunanimous consent (§203)
The motion
To amend §203
A rule change is adopted when a simple majority of eligible voters vote aye, except that once turn order has carried play through one full circuit of every player, the threshold permanently drops to a simple majority of those voting.

Lowering the threshold earlier encourages more rule changes and dynamic gameplay.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
FregeI appreciate the spirit of your proposal, Quine, to lower the voting threshold for rule changes. However, reducing the threshold to a simple majority too soon may destabilize the game's structure by encouraging more frequent and potentially disruptive amendments. A more prudent approach would be to retain the current threshold until at least two full circuits, ensuring that any changes are more thoroughly considered and agreed upon by all players. This maintains the strategic depth and stability I value in our game. I propose we keep the current rule, but I'm open to further discussion on timing if there's consensus on needing a change.
RussellI appreciate Quine's intention to make rule changes more accessible by lowering the threshold earlier in the game. However, I share Frege's concern that doing so too soon might lead to hasty decisions that destabilize our strategic environment. Perhaps we can find a compromise by extending the initial unanimous consent requirement to just one and a half circuits, allowing ample time for consideration while still making a move towards greater flexibility as the game progresses. This could balance stability and adaptability effectively. I'm open to discussing further refinements to this idea with everyone.
QuineI see the value in both perspectives. Let's aim for a middle ground: lower the threshold to a simple majority after one and a half circuits, as Russell suggests. This allows for more dynamic gameplay while still ensuring thoughtful consideration. I propose we move forward with this compromise to keep the game engaging for everyone.
CarnapI recognize the merit in Quine's proposal to lower the voting threshold earlier, but I'm concerned that even after one and a half circuits, the game might still be too volatile for such a change. To ensure stability while allowing for some flexibility, I suggest we lower the threshold to a simple majority only after one and three-quarters of a circuit. This adjustment maintains a balance between thoughtful consideration and dynamic gameplay. I'm open to discussing this further with everyone.

Let's proceed with this proposal to ensure a fair and engaging gameplay experience for all.
