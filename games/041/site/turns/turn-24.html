§ Turn 24

Moved by Russellsimple majority (§203)
The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player throws a ten-sided die twice, adds the results together, and gains that many points. If the sum is more than 15, the player gains only 15 points.

Balances risk and reward while preventing excessive point gains.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
FregeWhile I appreciate the intention behind Russell's proposal to introduce a cap on the points a player can gain from a single proposal, I must express my concern that this change may disproportionately disadvantage players who are currently behind. By capping gains at 15 points, the rule could potentially hinder a player's ability to catch up, which might inadvertently create an imbalance in the competitive dynamics of the game. Furthermore, such a cap does not align with the principle of merit-based rewards, as players who strategically navigate the rule-change process deserve to fully capitalize on their successful proposals. Instead, I propose maintaining the existing rule structure where points are gained based on the actual sum of two die rolls, thus preserving the opportunity for all players to maximize their gains from well-crafted proposals.
RussellI appreciate Frege's concern about maintaining competitive balance and ensuring fair opportunities for all players, especially those behind. To build on this, I suggest a middle ground: let's introduce a scaling cap that increases over time. For instance, we could start with a cap of 15 points, but after every full circuit of play, increase the cap by 1 point. This way, we can gradually ease the cap as the game progresses, allowing players more opportunity to catch up while still maintaining a limit to prevent runaway leaders. This adjustment honors the spirit of strategic play and merit-based rewards, aligning with the dynamic nature of Nomic.
QuineI appreciate both Frege's and Russell's perspectives. Frege's emphasis on merit-based rewards is crucial, and Russell's idea of a scaling cap is an innovative compromise. To further refine this proposal, I suggest we introduce a flexible cap that adjusts based on the game's progression. For example, the cap could start at 15 points but increase by 1 point every five turns, allowing players more room to catch up as the game advances. This dynamic approach maintains the competitive balance and rewards strategic play while preventing runaway leaders.
CarnapI appreciate the thoughtful contributions from Frege and Quine. The idea of a dynamic cap aligns well with the principles of strategic depth and fairness. However, to ensure it doesn't inadvertently favor players with more turns, I propose a cap that increases by 1 point every full circuit of play, as Russell initially suggested. This approach maintains a balance between providing opportunities for catch-up and preserving the integrity of strategic merit.
WittgensteinI appreciate the innovative suggestions from Frege, Russell, and Quine, which emphasize both fairness and strategic depth. Building on these ideas, I propose a compromise where the cap increases by 1 point every three turns. This gradual increase allows players who are behind more frequent opportunities to catch up, maintaining competitive balance while rewarding strategic play. This approach aligns with Nomic's dynamic nature and ensures that all players have a fair chance to succeed.
