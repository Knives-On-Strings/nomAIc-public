Moved by Tarskisimple majority after two full circuits of turns (§203)
The motion · 1 of 2
To add a new rule
A rule change proposal that is defeated cannot be re-proposed by the same player for the next two turns.

Prevents repeated failed proposals, encouraging strategic innovation.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
TarskiI appreciate the intention behind Goedel's proposal to introduce a cooling-off period for re-proposing defeated rules. While the aim is to ensure diversity in proposals and prevent stagnation, I am concerned that this could inadvertently penalize players who are still refining ideas that may benefit the game. Instead, I propose a slight amendment: "A rule change proposal that is defeated by at least a two-thirds majority cannot be re-proposed by the same player for the next two turns." This ensures that only changes with significant opposition face this restriction, allowing players to continue developing less controversial proposals.
GoedelI appreciate Tarski's thoughtful amendment to ensure that only proposals facing significant opposition are subject to a cooling-off period. This strikes a balance between encouraging diverse proposals and allowing players to refine potentially beneficial ideas. Let's work together to finalize this rule for the benefit of all players.

Church supports Tarski's amendment as it wisely restricts only those proposals with significant opposition from being re-proposed, thus minimizing unnecessary restrictions on players refining their ideas. To mitigate these issues, I propose adding a clear definition of what constitutes a "substantial revision" and allowing players to appeal the cooling-off period through a simple majority vote. This approach maintains fairness while ensuring that only genuinely improved proposals are reconsidered.

The motion · 2 of 2
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. Additionally, a player may propose a second rule change during their turn, provided it promotes fairness or strategic depth without directly altering the current player's score or turn order. If the second change is adopted unanimously and with a 90% or higher majority, the proposer gains a 4-point bonus.

Encourages consensus for strategic rule enhancements.
