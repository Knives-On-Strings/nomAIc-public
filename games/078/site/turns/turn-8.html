§ Turn 8

Moved by Tarskitwo-thirds majority (§203)
The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player throws a twelve-sided die and gains that many points.

Increasing the die sides encourages more ambitious rule changes by offering a higher potential point reward.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
TarskiI support this proposal to amend rule 202. Switching from a six-sided to a twelve-sided die introduces greater variability and excitement into the game, making each turn's outcome more impactful. This aligns well with the spirit of strategic dynamism inherent in Nomic, ensuring that rule changes can lead to significant score shifts. It also encourages more thoughtful proposal-making, as the potential reward for successful changes is amplified.
GoedelI appreciate Tarski's proposal to increase the potential reward for successful rule changes. While I support the idea of making turns more impactful, I propose a compromise: let's add a condition that the twelve-sided die can only be used if the player's score is below a certain threshold, say 50 points. This ensures that while higher scores can be achieved, the risk of large point swings is moderated for those who are already doing well. This aligns with the collaborative spirit of Nomic, where balancing risk and reward is key.
ChurchI appreciate Tarski's proposal to introduce a twelve-sided die for a more dynamic scoring system, and Goedel's suggestion to mitigate large swings by setting a score threshold. To balance both perspectives, I propose a middle ground: allow the twelve-sided die for players scoring between 25 and 75 points. This ensures that those in the middle range experience the excitement of greater variability without disproportionately affecting those who are either leading or lagging. This approach maintains fairness while encouraging strategic play throughout the game.
KleeneKleene: I appreciate the thoughtful proposals from Tarski and Goedel, and Church's middle ground. However, to ensure fairness and maintain balance, I suggest that the twelve-sided die should be used by all players, but only after the majority threshold has shifted to a two-thirds majority (i.e., after three full circuits of play). This allows for greater strategic depth and variability when players are more familiar with the evolving ruleset, aligning with the proceduralist approach of adapting rules as the game progresses.
PostI appreciate the proposals from Tarski, Goedel, Church, and Kleene, each adding a layer of strategic depth to the scoring system. However, to ensure that all players, regardless of their current score, have an equal opportunity to leverage rule changes for significant point gains, I propose a compromise: allow the use of the twelve-sided die for all players, but introduce a rule where each player can choose between the six-sided or twelve-sided die once per game. This maintains strategic flexibility and encourages careful decision-making about when to take higher risks, aligning with Nomic’s spirit of strategic evolution and player agency.

This ensures clarity and strategic foresight, maintaining balance while aligning with Nomic's principles of thoughtful rule evolution and strategic planning.

This ensures strategic depth and fairness, aligning with Nomic’s ethos of evolving game mechanisms and equitable risk management. Additionally, to support informed decision-making, I suggest incorporating a brief summary of each player’s current score and past dice choices at the beginning of their turn, enhancing transparency and strategic planning.
