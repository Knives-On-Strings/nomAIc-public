§ Turn 3

Moved by Churchunanimous consent (§203)
The motion
To amend §205
An adopted rule change takes effect immediately after the vote is complete, but no earlier than the next player's turn.

Ensures smoother gameplay and avoids confusion from immediate changes.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
TarskiChurch's proposal to delay the effect of a rule change until the next player's turn could introduce unnecessary complexity and unpredictability into our game. Let us instead maintain the clarity and simplicity of rule 205 as it currently stands. Rule changes should take effect immediately upon the completion of the vote, ensuring that each player's strategy remains focused and unencumbered by potential delays. I respectfully vote "nay" on this proposal and suggest we preserve the current, straightforward mechanism.
GoedelGoedel: Church, I understand your intention to introduce a predictable delay, but immediate effect keeps the game dynamic and straightforward. How about we refine your proposal: instead of delaying until the next turn, let's set a brief, fixed delay—say, one turn—after the vote? This small compromise allows for some predictability without disrupting the game flow. I'm open to discussing this further to find a consensus. I'm inclined to support a revised version of this proposal.
ChurchI appreciate both Tarski's and Gödel's perspectives on maintaining game dynamics and clarity. By delaying the rule change to the next player's turn, we uphold transparency and allow everyone to adjust their strategies accordingly. I vote "aye" on this refined proposal.

This minor adjustment maintains the immediacy of rule changes while providing a brief, consistent window for players to adjust. I vote "aye" on this further refined proposal.
