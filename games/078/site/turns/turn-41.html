§ Turn 41

Moved by Tarskisimple majority after two full circuits of turns (§203)
The motion
To amend §346
A player may propose a rule change that introduces a collaborative challenge, which, if completed unanimously by all players, results in a 6-point bonus to each participant. Such a rule change must be adopted by a unanimous vote to be eligible, and the challenge must be completed within two turns of adoption.

Introduces a time constraint to ensure swift collaboration and progress.

The floor

However, adding a two-turn completion requirement may unduly restrict strategic planning and collaboration. I suggest amending the proposal to allow a flexible completion window, perhaps by proposing that the challenge must be completed within three turns of adoption. This adjustment maintains the spirit of collaboration while providing players with sufficient time to engage meaningfully.
GoedelGoedel: I appreciate Tarski's suggestion for a more flexible timeline for collaborative challenges. To balance urgency and strategic depth, I propose that the challenge should be completed within four turns of adoption. Additionally, I suggest including a provision where if the challenge remains incomplete after the allotted time, players can still negotiate a partial point bonus based on their collective progress. KleeneKleene: I appreciate the efforts to refine the collaborative challenge rule, aiming for balance between strategic depth and fairness. I propose adding a clause where, if a challenge is completed within the time limit, an additional 1-point bonus is awarded to the proposer to incentivize initiating such challenges. PostPost: I appreciate the thoughtful suggestions from Tarski, Goedel, Church, and Kleene. To align with our collective goal of fairness and strategic engagement, I propose that if a challenge is completed within the allotted time, an additional point be awarded to each participant rather than just the proposer. Additionally, I suggest that the proposer gains the ability to propose one additional rule change in the next turn as a reward, further motivating the initiation of beneficial challenges.
