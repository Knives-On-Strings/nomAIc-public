§ Turn 12

Moved by Tarskitwo-thirds majority (§203)
The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player throws a six-sided die and gains that many points.

To reduce variance and make the game more strategic.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
TarskiI appreciate the proposal's aim to maintain the strategic depth of proposing rule changes, but I would suggest a compromise: "A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. If the proposal is adopted, the proposing player throws a ten-sided die and gains that many points." This maintains a higher level of strategic impact than a six-sided die while reducing the maximum points gained from the twelve-sided die, encouraging more thoughtful proposals. I hope you'll consider this amendment.
GoedelI appreciate Tarski's suggestion for a ten-sided die, which strikes a good balance between reducing the maximum points and maintaining strategic depth. To further enhance fairness, how about we add a clause that allows a player who loses a vote to gain one point as a consolation? This way, even those who don't win a proposal still have a chance to score, which might encourage more participation and constructive proposals.
ChurchI appreciate Goedel's suggestion for a consolation point, which could encourage participation. However, I'm concerned that it might lead to a situation where players are incentivized to propose changes they don't fully support just to gain that consolation point. Instead, I propose we add a clause that allows a player who loses a vote to gain two points if they can demonstrate that their proposal was blocked by less than a two-thirds majority. This way, we maintain strategic depth while still rewarding thoughtful proposals.
KleeneKleene: I appreciate the discussion so far, especially the focus on maintaining strategic depth. While the ten-sided die seems like a reasonable compromise, I suggest we add a clause that requires a minimum of a two-thirds majority for a proposal to pass, even during the initial phase of the game. This would ensure that only well-considered proposals are adopted, promoting thoughtful deliberation and preventing any player from gaining an early, significant advantage. I believe this will align with our principles of fairness and procedural integrity.
