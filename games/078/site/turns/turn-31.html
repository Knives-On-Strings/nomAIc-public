Moved by Goedelsimple majority after two full circuits of turns (§203)
The motion · 1 of 2
To add a new rule
A player may propose a rule change that increases the target score by 10 points. If adopted unanimously, the proposer gains 5 points.

This change adds strategic depth and rewards collaborative, unanimous agreement.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
TarskiI appreciate the intent to make the game more dynamic by adjusting the target score, but I have some concerns. Allowing such changes can make the game less stable and more unpredictable, which may not align with our established preference for measured amendments. If we wish to pursue this, perhaps we could include a condition that such a rule change must be debated for a minimum period or require a higher majority for adoption to ensure thoughtful consideration. I am open to discussing these or other conditions further. For now, I propose a counter-amendment: "A player may propose a rule change that increases the target score, but it must pass with a three-fourths majority." This ensures that any such significant change is carefully considered by all players. I am willing to negotiate on these terms to gain support for this counter-amendment. PASS
GoedelI appreciate Tarski's cautious approach to maintaining the game's stability and fairness. I agree that significant changes like altering the target score require careful deliberation. To balance both our interests, I propose a compromise: a rule change to increase the target score should require a two-thirds majority, coupled with a mandatory debate period of three minutes. This ensures both thoughtful consideration and a reasonable threshold for adoption. I am open to further negotiation on these terms to find common ground.
ChurchI appreciate both Tarski's and Goedel's efforts to ensure stability and thoughtful consideration in this proposal. While I understand the intent to make the game more dynamic, I worry that increasing the target score might disproportionately affect players who are currently closer to winning, potentially prolonging the game unnecessarily. To address these concerns, I suggest we include an additional clause: "A rule change to increase the target score can only be proposed once per player throughout the game." This limitation ensures that the game remains competitive without frequent and potentially disruptive changes. I am open to negotiating further on these terms to reach a mutually agreeable solution.
KleeneI appreciate both Tarski's and Goedel's contributions towards ensuring thoughtful consideration of such a significant change, and Church's suggestion to maintain competitive balance by limiting the frequency of score increases. However, I have concerns about the potential for this rule to disrupt the strategic landscape of the game, especially for those of us who are still building our strategies from the ground up. To address these concerns while acknowledging the intent behind the proposal, I propose an additional safeguard: "Any rule change to increase the target score must also include a provision that reduces the threshold for adopting future rule changes by 5%." This adjustment would serve as a compensatory mechanism to ensure that the game remains dynamic and that all players have a fair chance to adapt to new conditions. I am open to further negotiation on these terms to ensure that any change we adopt is both fair and strategically sound.
PostI appreciate the thoughtful contributions from Tarski, Goedel, Church, and Kleene, each of whom has highlighted important aspects of fairness and strategic balance in this proposal. The introduction of a higher threshold and a limit on the number of proposals per player are prudent measures to ensure stability. To further bolster these safeguards, I propose we add a clause: "A rule change that increases the target score cannot be proposed in the final five turns of the game." This will prevent any last-minute disruptions to the game's conclusion. I am open to negotiation on these terms to find a balanced resolution.

The motion · 2 of 2
To amend §329
A player may propose a rule change that allows for an additional strategic action during their turn, but only if it passes a unanimous vote among all players and does not directly affect the proposer's score.

This amendment ensures fairness by preventing the proposer from gaining an immediate advantage.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
TarskiI appreciate Goedel's proposal to amend [329], which aims to prevent proposals that could unduly advantage the proposer. However, the restriction against affecting the proposer's score could unduly limit strategic rule changes that promote fairness. To address both concerns, I suggest the amendment be further refined to include a clause that any rule change must be vetted by at least two other players before voting. This way, it ensures impartiality while still allowing strategic depth.
GoedelI appreciate Tarski's suggestion to involve other players in the vetting process, as it adds an additional layer of impartiality to rule changes. To balance strategic flexibility with fairness, I propose that any vetted rule change also be subject to a 24-hour reflection period before voting, allowing players to consider its implications fully. This period would ensure thoughtful participation without stalling the game's progress.
ChurchI appreciate Tarski's and Goedel's suggestions for enhancing the fairness of rule changes while maintaining strategic depth. To further ensure balanced gameplay, I propose that any rule change requiring a unanimous vote should also include a clause mandating that it cannot be proposed more than once per game by the same player. This would prevent repeated attempts that could disrupt the game's flow while still allowing for meaningful strategic innovations.
KleeneI appreciate Church's proposal to limit each player to a single unanimous vote per game, which prevents repeated disruptions while fostering strategic creativity. To ensure this rule is not exploited, I suggest adding a requirement that any such rule change must be reviewed by a neutral third party, like the game's arbiter, to confirm its fairness and strategic value before being allowed to vote.
PostPost: I appreciate the thoughtful contributions from Tarski, Goedel, Church, and Kleene. To address the need for balance and strategic depth, I propose that any rule change under [329] must include a provision for a post-vote review, ensuring its impact aligns with the game's fairness principles. This review could be conducted by a panel of two players chosen randomly, providing a check without the need for an external arbiter. Let's ensure our changes foster innovation while maintaining integrity.
