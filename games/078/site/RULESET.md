# The Statute

_As at turn 50._

## Entrenched

**§101.** Every player must, at all times, abide by the ruleset currently in force — including every change adopted since play began.
**§102.** Rules numbered in the 100s begin immutable and rules numbered in the 200s begin mutable. Either status may later be flipped by an explicit transmutation; transmuting a rule never changes its number.
**§103.** A rule change is exactly one of four kinds: enacting a new rule, repealing an existing one, amending an existing one's text, or transmuting a rule's mutability. No other kind of rule change exists.
**§104.** Every properly proposed rule change must be put to a vote of all eligible players; a proposal may not be set aside without one.
**§105.** Every player is an eligible voter, and every eligible voter must cast a vote on every proposal that comes to a vote.
**§106.** A proposal must be written down in the exact words that will be voted on. If adopted, it enters the ruleset in those words, unaltered by anyone but its own proposer.
**§107.** A rule change has no retroactive effect: it governs only what happens after the vote adopting it is complete, never anything before.
**§108.** Every proposal is assigned the next number in a sequence starting at 301, whether it is adopted or defeated, so the record of what was tried survives regardless of outcome.
**§109.** Transmuting a rule's mutability requires the unanimous assent of all eligible voters, and must be proposed and voted on explicitly as a transmutation — never as a side effect of some other change.
**§110.** Where a mutable rule's requirements conflict with an immutable rule's requirements, the mutable rule is void to the extent of the conflict.
**§111.** Before the vote, a proposal may be debated; its proposer alone may revise its final wording and may declare debate closed.
**§112.** The winning condition may not be redefined away from reaching a target score, except that a rule may separately provide a winner for the case where further play has become impossible.
**§113.** Any player may forfeit the game at any time. Forfeiting carries no penalty worse than losing outright.
**§114.** At least one mutable rule must remain in the ruleset at all times, and no rule change may render every future rule change impermissible.
**§115.** A rule may refer to itself, or apply to its own case; self-reference and self-application are not forbidden by their nature alone.
**§116.** Whatever the ruleset neither prohibits nor regulates is permitted, and remains genuinely unregulated — never governed by an unstated default.

## Mutable

**§201.** Players take one fixed turn order, set before play begins, which may be altered by a unanimously adopted rule change. Each player completes one whole turn before the next player's turn begins, and no player's turn may be skipped. Every player's score starts at zero.
**§202.** A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly one rule change, which is debated and then voted on by every player. Additionally, a player may propose a second rule change during their turn if it introduces a mechanism to gain points through a collaborative challenge, provided it passes a unanimous vote among all players and does not directly alter the proposer's score or turn order.
**§203.** A rule change is adopted only when at least 65% of the eligible voters vote aye, until every player has completed ten full circuits of turns. Thereafter, the threshold drops permanently to a simple majority.
**§204.** A rule change may be adopted with a two-thirds majority, and a player who voted against an adopted change gains 5 points and remains bound by the change exactly as if they had voted for it. Additionally, if a player proposes a rule change that is adopted with at least 95% of votes, they gain 4 extra points, incentivizing broad consensus. Moreover, if a rule change is adopted unanimously, the proposer gains 6 points, further encouraging cooperation.
**§205.** An adopted rule change takes effect immediately after the vote is complete, but no earlier than the next player's turn.
**§206.** A proposal that fails to be adopted is recorded as defeated. Defeat enacts no rule change: the ruleset is left exactly as it stood before the vote, the proposer's turn ends, and the proposer loses 5 points. Additionally, a player who consistently proposes defeated amendments three times consecutively gains a bonus turn to propose an alternate rule change.
**§207.** Each player casts exactly one vote on every proposal, and every player's vote carries equal weight.
**§208.** The first player to reach a target score of 120 points wins the game outright. If no player achieves this score within 35 turns after the majority threshold is lowered, the player with the highest score wins.
**§209.** The ruleset may never hold more than 22 mutable rules at one time.
**§210.** Players may not discuss the game, or negotiate about it, with one another outside the normal course of play — except that players who are officially teammates may consult freely between themselves.
**§211.** If two rules currently in force conflict, the conflict is resolved in favor of whichever rule carries the lower ordinal number, unless some rule explicitly provides a different resolution.
**§212.** Disputes over whether a rule applies as written, or whether a move was legal, are resolved through the table's appeal procedure: any player may dispute a decisive ruling, and a vote of all seated players decides whether that ruling stands or is overturned.
**§213.** If the ruleset is changed so that further play is impossible, or if a move's legality cannot be determined with finality, or if a move appears equally legal and illegal by the best reasoning available and not overruled, then the first player unable to complete a turn wins the game outright. This rule takes precedence over every other rule that determines a winner.
**§316.** A player's score cannot drop below zero under any rule change; if a rule change would cause a negative score, the score is set to zero instead.
**§318.** A proposal to repeal a mutable rule must first be debated for a minimum of three minutes before voting begins.
**§324.** A player who proposes a rule change that is adopted without opposition gains 3 points. This bonus does not apply if the player voted against the change.
**§329.** A player may propose a rule change that allows for an additional strategic action during their turn, but only if it passes a unanimous vote among all players and does not directly or indirectly affect the proposer's score.
**§338.** A player may propose a rule change that increases the target score by 10 points. If adopted unanimously, the proposer gains 5 points.
**§340.** A rule change proposal that is defeated cannot be re-proposed by the same player for the next two turns.
**§344.** A player may propose a rule change that allows for a one-time exchange of points between players, provided it passes a unanimous vote among all players and does not directly or indirectly benefit the proposer.
**§346.** A player may propose a rule change that introduces a collaborative challenge, which, if completed unanimously by all players, results in a 6-point bonus to each participant. Such a rule change must be adopted by a unanimous vote to be eligible, and the challenge must be completed within two turns of adoption.
**§348.** A player may propose a rule change that allows for a strategic pause, where any player can declare a pause on rule changes for one turn. If adopted unanimously, the player proposing the pause gains 2 points.
