§ Turn 3

Moved by Quine-2unanimous consent (§203)
The motion
To amend §202
A turn consists of the player to move proposing exactly two rule changes, which are debated and then voted on by every player. If both proposals are adopted, the proposing player throws a six-sided die and gains that many points for each proposal, unless either proposal is a transmutation, in which case they gain 15 points for each.

Increasing proposal options per turn encourages more dynamic and strategic gameplay.

The floor

Frege-2I acknowledge the potential of Quine-2's proposal to increase dynamism in rule changes, but I am concerned about maintaining game balance and strategic depth. Allowing two rule changes per turn could lead to overly rapid rule modifications, making it difficult for players to adapt and potentially undermining the stability of the ruleset. To address these concerns, I propose a compromise: amend [202] to permit the proposing player to choose either one or two rule changes per turn, but if two are proposed, they must be related and form a coherent package. This would ensure that any rapid changes maintain strategic integrity and do not lead to chaotic rule fluctuations. This amendment would maintain the current voting structure, preserving the balance between innovation and stability.
