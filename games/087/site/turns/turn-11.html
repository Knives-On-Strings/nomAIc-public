The motion
To add a new rule
A player who is penalized under rule 206 for an illegal proposal (because it did not match their pre-declaration) may, at the start of their next turn, propose a correction: an exact amendment to rule 309 that would make their earlier proposal legal had it been in force at that time. If that correction proposal is adopted, the player regains the 10 points they lost from the illegal proposal, and no further points are gained from the adoption of the correction itself.

This provides a remedy for honest mistakes under the strict pre-declaration rule, encouraging careful rulecraft rather than permanent penalty.

The floor
Discussion · round 1

ZhuXiI understand your concern about undermining rule 309's purpose, Laozi, but a one-time correction aligns with fairness by allowing genuine oversights to be remedied without rewarding negligence — provided the correction itself yields no further points.

However, ZhuXi's point about fairness for genuine oversights is valid — provided the correction yields no points, it remedies without reward. I could support this if the correction also required unanimous consent, preserving the high threshold for such exceptions.
DongZhongshuI oppose any rule that undermines the binding force of pre-declaration. Penalties under rule 309 exist precisely to ensure proposals align with stated intent; allowing correction erodes that discipline.
ShaoYongI see merit in ZhuXi’s fairness argument — a correction without extra points can remedy honest oversights while keeping 309’s discipline. But I’d add a circuit limit: only one correction per player every two full circuits, to prevent abuse.
