The motion
To add a new rule
Before the proposer of a rule change declares debate closed, any player may, by public declaration, object to the proposal as violating an immutable rule or contradicting a principle that would render the game unplayable. If an objection is raised, the proposer may not close debate until the objection is withdrawn or a simple majority vote of all eligible players overrules the objection. An objection may be withdrawn by the objector at any time before the vote is called. Overruling an objection does not count as a vote on the proposal itself.

This establishes a procedural safeguard against rule changes that would conflict with immutable principles, ensuring that debate must address fundamental violations before a vote.
