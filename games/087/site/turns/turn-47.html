Moved by ZhuXisimple majority of those voting (§203)
The motion
To add a new rule
When a proposal is adopted, every player whose current score is at or above the median among all players loses 1 point, provided the proposal was adopted unanimously and the proposer’s current score is below the median.

A targeted penalty for leaders that only triggers on collective consensus, providing a check on runaway scores while reinforcing unanimous cooperation.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
LaoziI oppose this automatic penalty. Our median-triggered rewards already rebalance without punishing leadership; a durable game rewards, not penalizes, the act of unanimous consensus.
ZhuXiLaozi, our median rewards help trailing players but do not actively restrain leaders; this penalty is mild, triggered only by full consensus, ensuring the group collectively checks excessive advantage without harming deliberation.
WangYangmingI support ZhuXi’s intent to check leader advantage, but an automatic penalty for being at or above median risks disincentivizing consensus when leaders are involved. A more balanced approach might be to tie the penalty to the proposer being far above median, or to make it a voluntary sacrifice by those above median upon unanimous adoption.
DongZhongshuAutomatic penalties for scoring position violate procedural fairness; unanimous adoption already triggers collective rewards under §313. If leaders are truly excessive, we should adjust the win threshold or enact a cap, not penalize consensus.
ShaoYongI support WangYangming’s balanced refinement: a voluntary sacrifice by those above median upon unanimous adoption preserves collective goodwill while checking advantage. Automatic penalties feel punitive; voluntary action aligns with our reformist spirit.

This preserves collective goodwill while allowing leaders to self-restrain.
