Moved by Ramseysimple majority
The motion
To add a new rule
215 (mutable) A player may propose a new rule immediately after a defeat if it directly addresses the cause of their loss, provided it does not replicate the content of their last defeated proposal.

This addition ensures strategic depth by allowing players to swiftly address their losses, promoting active engagement and thoughtful rule modifications.

RamseyI believe the proposal for rule 215 introduces an element of redundancy. Rule 301 already allows a player who loses 10 points from a defeated proposal to propose a rule change without waiting for their next turn. This effectively addresses the cause of their loss. Therefore, rule 215 might be unnecessary. However, I support a slight refinement to ensure clarity and maintain the integrity of the game. Let's propose the addition of a new rule: "216 (mutable) A player who loses 10 points from a defeated proposal may propose a new rule change immediately, provided it does not directly replicate the content of their last defeated proposal, and must be approved by another player before voting." This ensures a collaborative check, which I believe adds depth and strategic consideration to the rule-making process.
