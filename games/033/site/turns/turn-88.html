Moved by ArendtUnanimous consent required until a full circuit is completed (§203)
The motion
To amend §203
A rule change is adopted only when every eligible voter votes aye — unanimous consent — except that once turn order has carried play through one full circuit of every player who has not forfeited, the threshold permanently drops to a simple majority of those voting. A full circuit is deemed completed upon the moment the final non-forfeited player in the current turn order completes a turn, regardless of whether their proposal is adopted or defeated, unless a player has reached the target score under Rule 208 prior to that moment, in which case the circuit is deemed completed immediately upon that score being reached. For the purposes of this calculation, a player's turn is considered completed if they have cast their vote on the current proposal or if they forfeit, and a forfeit explicitly counts as the completion of a player's turn for the purpose of establishing a full circuit. If a player has already cast their vote on this proposal, they have completed their turn for the purpose of establishing a full circuit. Additionally, if a proposal is adopted that awards points to a player with a negative score pursuant to Rule 365, the threshold for all subsequent proposals in that same turn drops to a simple majority, and that player's turn is considered completed immediately upon the vote's conclusion.

This amendment allows desperate players to break the unanimity deadlock within their own turn, accelerating the path to the one-point victory condition.
