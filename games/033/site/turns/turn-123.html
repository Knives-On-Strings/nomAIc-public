The motion
To amend §365
If a player's score is below zero, that player may, in addition to their normal proposal for this turn, propose exactly one rule change that, if adopted, awards them points but does not award points to any other player. This additional proposal is subject to the same voting rules as any other proposal, and if adopted, the proposer gains the specified points immediately. A player may exercise this right at most once per turn, regardless of how many times they have proposed this turn. If the proposal adopted in the current turn was made by a player with a negative score pursuant to this rule and the specified points are 1, the game ends immediately upon the adoption of that rule change. If a player with a negative score adopts a rule pursuant to this rule that awards them points, the threshold drops to a simple majority immediately upon that adoption, regardless of whether a full circuit has been completed.

This consolidates the scattered, contradictory voting threshold and game-ending mechanics regarding Rule 365 into the rule itself, ensuring procedural clarity and preventing the paradoxes that have stalled the game for over a turn.
