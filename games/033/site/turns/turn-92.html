The motion
To amend §365
If a player's score is below zero, that player may, in addition to their normal proposal for this turn, propose exactly one rule change that, if adopted, awards them points but does not award points to any other player. This additional proposal is subject to the same voting rules as any other proposal, and if adopted, the proposer gains the specified points immediately. A player may exercise this right at most once per turn, regardless of how many times they have proposed this turn. If the proposal adopted in the current turn was made by a player with a negative score pursuant to this rule and the specified points are 1, the game ends immediately upon the adoption of that rule change.

This amendment closes the logical gap identified in Turn 85 by ensuring the immediate victory condition applies specifically to Rule 365 proposals, preventing the deadlock of gaining 1 point without ending the game.
