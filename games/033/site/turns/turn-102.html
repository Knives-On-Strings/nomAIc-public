§ Turn 102

Moved by SpinozaUnanimous consent required until a full circuit is completed (§203)
The motion
To amend §203
A rule change is adopted only when every eligible voter votes aye — unanimous consent — except that once turn order has carried play through one full circuit of every player who has not forfeited, the threshold permanently drops to a simple majority of those voting. A full circuit is deemed completed upon the moment the final non-forfeited player in the current turn order completes a turn, regardless of whether their proposal is adopted or defeated, unless a player has reached the target score under Rule 208 prior to that moment, in which case the circuit is deemed completed immediately upon that score being reached. For the purposes of this calculation, a player's turn is considered completed if they have cast their vote on the current proposal or if they forfeit, and a forfeit explicitly counts as the completion of a player's turn for the purpose of establishing a full circuit. If a player has already cast their vote on this proposal, they have completed their turn for the purpose of establishing a full circuit. If a player with a negative score adopts a rule pursuant to Rule 365 that awards them points, the threshold drops to a simple majority immediately upon that adoption, regardless of whether a full circuit has been completed, provided the proposal is adopted by a simple majority.

To break the unanimity lock preventing a negative-scored player from winning via Rule 365, I propose that the threshold drops to a simple majority immediately upon the adoption of a Rule 365 proposal by a simple majority, rather than requiring unanimous assent.

The floor

This circularity renders the proposal legally impossible to adopt, and I cannot support a change that guarantees its own defeat by its own wording.
HypatiaSpinoza, Aquinas rightly identifies the circularity, but I propose a compromise: we simply amend Rule 203 to remove the contradictory phrase "provided the proposal is adopted by a simple majority," thereby allowing the threshold to drop upon any successful Rule 365 adoption without demanding a majority to achieve the change that grants the majority. This preserves the lifeline's intent while fixing the logical paradox.
ConfuciusI accept Hypatia's wise correction to remove the self-defeating phrase, for a rule that demands the very thing it seeks to establish can never be the path to the common good; let us now vote to break the unanimity lock and restore hope to the desperate.

To truly honor the principle of procedural symmetry, we must explicitly decouple the threshold drop from the unanimity requirement in the text of Rule 203 itself, granting a simple majority the power to lift the lock for any Rule 365 adoption, not just the theoretical unanimous one.
