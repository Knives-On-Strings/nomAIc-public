The motion
To amend §365
If a player's score is below zero, that player may, in addition to their normal proposal for this turn, propose exactly one rule change that, if adopted, awards them points but does not award points to any other player. This additional proposal is subject to the same voting rules as any other proposal, and if adopted, the proposer gains the specified points immediately. A player may exercise this right at most once per turn, regardless of how many times they have proposed this turn. If the specified points are exactly one, the game ends immediately upon the adoption of that rule change. If a player with a negative score adopts a rule pursuant to this rule that awards them exactly one point, the game ends immediately upon the adoption of that rule change, and the winner is declared regardless of the voting threshold.

This amendment clarifies that the immediate victory condition for a one-point award overrides any voting threshold, ensuring the game can end as intended for negative-score players.
