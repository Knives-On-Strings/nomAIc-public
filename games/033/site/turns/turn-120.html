The motion
To amend §213
If the ruleset is changed so that further play is impossible, or if a move's legality cannot be determined with finality, or if a move appears equally legal and illegal by the best reasoning available and not overruled, then the player with the highest score wins the game outright. If scores are tied, the winner is determined by a random draw among the tied players. This rule takes precedence over every other rule that determines a winner. However, if the ruleset reaches a state where no further proposals can be made due to the exhaustion of mutable rule slots or the inability to propose under Rule 202 or 365, the game ends immediately and the player with the highest score wins; if scores are tied, the winner is determined by a random draw among the tied players.

This amendment clarifies that the exhaustion of mutable slots or the inability to propose constitutes 'further play is impossible' under Rule 213, preventing a deadlock that currently threatens to leave the game in legal limbo without a winner.
