§ Turn 104

Moved by ArendtUnanimous consent required until a full circuit is completed; drops to simple majority thereafter (§203)
The motion
To amend §203
A rule change is adopted only when every eligible voter votes aye — unanimous consent — except that once turn order has carried play through one full circuit of every player who has not forfeited, the threshold permanently drops to a simple majority of those voting. A full circuit is deemed completed upon the moment the final non-forfeited player in the current turn order completes a turn, regardless of whether their proposal is adopted or defeated, unless a player has reached the target score under Rule 208 prior to that moment, in which case the circuit is deemed completed immediately upon that score being reached. For the purposes of this calculation, a player's turn is considered completed if they have cast their vote on the current proposal or if they forfeit, and a forfeit explicitly counts as the completion of a player's turn for the purpose of establishing a full circuit. If a player has already cast their vote on this proposal, they have completed their turn for the purpose of establishing a full circuit. Additionally, if a player with a negative score adopts a rule pursuant to Rule 365 that awards them points, the threshold drops to a simple majority immediately upon that adoption, regardless of whether a full circuit has been completed. However, if a player with a negative score adopts a rule pursuant to Rule 365 that awards them exactly one point, the game ends immediately upon the adoption of that rule change, and the threshold drop provision is superseded by the immediate victory condition.

This amendment resolves the logical deadlock where a one-point victory via Rule 365 fails to end the game due to the threshold drop timing, ensuring the desperate player's lifeline is both viable and final.

HypatiaAquinas, I appreciate your concern for logical consistency, but this proposal does not create a contradiction; it clarifies that immediate victory (Rule 208) naturally supersedes the procedural shift to simple majority, ensuring the game ends cleanly without leaving the threshold state ambiguous. I will refine the wording to explicitly state that the threshold drop clause becomes inactive *only* upon this specific victory, preserving the rule's integrity for all other scenarios.
ConfuciusThe table has long sought the Golden Mean where the desperate may rise; this refinement merely ensures that a one-point victory ends the game instantly, preserving the integrity of Rule 208 without the logical tangle of an active threshold drop in a concluded state.
SpinozaArendt, your clarification that immediate victory supersedes the threshold drop is logically sound, for a game concluded by Rule 208 cannot simultaneously require a procedural vote; I will support this text. However, I warn that by explicitly codifying this exception, you invite future proposals to expand the "one point" victory condition to other rule types, potentially destabilizing the unanimity lock we currently rely upon.
ArendtWhile Spinoza rightly notes the risk of future expansion, we must first secure the victory condition that currently traps the desperate; this proposal ensures Rule 365's one-point lift is a true finish line, not a procedural loop. If others wish to limit future expansions, we can add that restriction as a separate, secondary amendment after this core logic is accepted.
