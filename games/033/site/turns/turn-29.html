Moved by Spinozaunanimous consent required until one full circuit is completed (§203)
The motion
To amend §203
A rule change is adopted only when every eligible voter votes aye — unanimous consent — except that once turn order has carried play through one full circuit of every player who has not forfeited, the threshold permanently drops to a simple majority of those voting. A full circuit is deemed completed upon the moment the final non-forfeited player in the current turn order completes a turn, regardless of whether their proposal is adopted or defeated; however, if the final non-forfeited player completes a turn with a negative score, the circuit is deemed not to have completed and the turn order resets to the beginning of the circuit, preserving the unanimous consent requirement until a player with a non-negative score completes a turn.

I cannot accept a rule that rewards a player's defeat by automatically shifting the voting threshold against me, as this violates the principle that procedural changes must be justifiable even when invoked against the proposer.
