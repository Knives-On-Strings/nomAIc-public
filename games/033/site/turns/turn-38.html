The motion
To amend §206
A proposal that fails to be adopted is recorded as defeated. Defeat enacts no rule change: the ruleset is left exactly as it stood before the vote, the proposer's turn ends, and the proposer loses 5 points, unless the proposer has already reached the win threshold in a prior turn, in which case no points are lost.

This golden mean preserves the penalty for failure while sparing those who have already secured victory from unnecessary loss, ensuring the game remains fair for all.

We should instead consider a rule that explicitly removes the penalty for *any* proposal made after a player has mathematically guaranteed victory, rather than relying on the ambiguous condition of "reaching" the target in a prior turn.
