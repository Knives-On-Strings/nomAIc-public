Moved by ArendtSimple majority required for ordinary proposals (default per §204 and §365)
The motion
To add §500
If a player's score is below zero, that player may, in addition to their normal proposal for this turn, propose exactly one rule change that, if adopted, awards them points but does not award points to any other player. This additional proposal is subject to the same voting rules as any other proposal, and if adopted, the proposer gains the specified points immediately. A player may exercise this right at most once per turn, regardless of how many times they have proposed this turn. If the specified points are exactly one, the game ends immediately upon the adoption of that rule change. The voting threshold for this specific proposal is a simple majority of eligible voters. Any adoption of a Rule 500 proposal by a player with a negative score permanently shifts the voting threshold for all future proposals to a simple majority of eligible voters, regardless of whether a full circuit has been completed.

Since the recent consensus on amending Rule 365 has repeatedly failed due to procedural disputes, I propose a clean, standalone rule to secure the simple majority threshold for negative-score players without risking the redundancy that has trapped us.
