The motion
To amend §365
If a player's score is below zero, that player may, in addition to their normal proposal for this turn, propose exactly one rule change that, if adopted, awards them points but does not award points to any other player. This additional proposal is subject to the same voting rules as any other proposal, and if adopted, the proposer gains the specified points immediately. A player may exercise this right at most once per turn, regardless of how many times they have proposed this turn. If the specified points in such an adopted rule are exactly one, the game ends immediately upon the adoption of that rule change.

This amendment explicitly links the one-point victory condition to Rule 365, ensuring the desperate player has a genuine, immediate path to win without relying on ambiguous circuit thresholds.
