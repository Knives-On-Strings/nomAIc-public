Moved by Spinozaunanimous consent required until a full circuit is completed (§203)
The motion
To amend §203
A rule change is adopted only when every eligible voter votes aye — unanimous consent — except that once turn order has carried play through one full circuit of every player who has not forfeited, the threshold permanently drops to a simple majority of those voting. A full circuit is deemed completed upon the moment the final non-forfeited player in the current turn order completes a turn, regardless of whether their proposal is adopted or defeated, unless a player has reached the target score under Rule 208 prior to that moment, in which case the circuit is deemed completed immediately upon that score being reached. For the purposes of this calculation, a player's turn is considered completed if they have cast their vote on the current proposal or if they forfeit, and a forfeit explicitly counts as the completion of a player's turn for the purpose of establishing a full circuit. Furthermore, if any player's score reaches zero or above, the unanimous consent requirement is immediately suspended for all future proposals until that player's score falls below zero again.

To break the deadlock of negative scores preventing victory, I propose a temporary suspension of the unanimity requirement once any player escapes deficit, allowing the game to proceed without granting points to the proposer.
