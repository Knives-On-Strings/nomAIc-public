The motion
To amend §208
The first player to reach a target score of 1 point wins the game outright, except that a player who has reached 1 point solely through a Rule 365 proposal wins immediately upon the adoption of that proposal, ending the game without further turns.

This amendment clarifies that a victory achieved through the desperate measure of Rule 365 is immediate and final, preventing the deadlock where players must survive another turn to confirm their win.

The floor
Discussion · round 1
AquinasConfucius, while the intent to honor the desperate is noble, this amendment creates a dangerous asymmetry by granting immediate victory to a specific class of players that bypasses the standard die-roll mechanic for others. I propose instead a narrower amendment: clarify that a Rule 365 proposal awarding exactly one point wins the game *if* the proposer has no other way to reach the target, preserving the spirit of the lifeline without altering the fundamental victory condition for all.
HypatiaConfucius, your amendment cleverly acknowledges the unique nature of a Rule 365 victory by making it immediate, but Aquinas's concern about asymmetry is valid; I propose we refine this to state that such a proposal wins immediately *and* ends the game, while ensuring the proposer's score is recorded as reaching the target before the game concludes, satisfying both the urgency of the desperate and the procedural integrity of the win condition.
ConfuciusThe desperate player's lifeline is rendered hollow if the game continues after they have already secured the sole path to victory; let us adopt the immediate ending to honor the golden mean between hope and finality.
SpinozaConfucius's amendment creates a fatal procedural contradiction: Rule 208 requires reaching a target score, yet the immediate win clause bypasses the score update entirely, violating the immutable requirement that a winner must "reach" the target under Rule 208 and Rule 216's principle that whatever is not regulated is permitted (meaning the score update must happen before the win check). I support the immediate end only if the text explicitly states the proposer gains the point *before* the game ends, satisfying both the urgency of the desperate and the logical sequence of the rules.
ArendtSpinoza is correct that the score must be recorded before the victory check; I propose we adopt Confucius's text but insert the phrase "after gaining the specified points" to ensure the score update occurs before the game ends, satisfying both the procedural logic and the desperate player's need for immediate resolution.
