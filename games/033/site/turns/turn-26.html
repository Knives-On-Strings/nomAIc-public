Moved by Aquinasunanimous consent required until one full circuit is completed (§203)
The motion
To amend §203
A rule change is adopted only when every eligible voter votes aye — unanimous consent — except that once turn order has carried play through one full circuit of every player who has not forfeited, the threshold permanently drops to a simple majority of those voting. A full circuit is deemed completed upon the moment the final non-forfeited player in the current turn order completes a turn, regardless of whether their proposal is adopted or defeated, provided that no more than two consecutive turns have passed since the last time the threshold was checked and found not yet reached; if two consecutive turns pass without the threshold shifting, the threshold shifts automatically at the end of the second such turn.

I propose this amendment to prevent the game from stagnating under perpetual unanimity by instituting an automatic shift after a brief deadlock of two consecutive turns.

Instead of a majority vote to delay, let us adopt a "forced shift" trigger: if no shift occurs after two turns, the threshold drops automatically at the end of the second turn, ensuring the game's momentum is never held hostage by a single holdout's inertia.
