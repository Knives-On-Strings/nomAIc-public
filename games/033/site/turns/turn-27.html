Moved by Hypatiaunanimous consent required until one full circuit is completed (§203)
The motion
To amend §203
A rule change is adopted only when every eligible voter votes aye — unanimous consent — except that once turn order has carried play through one full circuit of every player who has not forfeited, the threshold permanently drops to a simple majority of those voting. A full circuit is deemed completed upon the moment the final non-forfeited player in the current turn order completes a turn, regardless of whether their proposal is adopted or defeated, provided that at least one proposal was successfully adopted during that circuit; if no proposal was adopted, the circuit continues until the next player completes a turn.

This prevents the game from being held hostage by a total stalemate where no one can vote 'aye' because the circuit condition itself requires a successful adoption, while preserving the consensus builders' intent to keep unanimity as the default.

I therefore oppose this amendment as it introduces a fatal deadlock where none existed before.
