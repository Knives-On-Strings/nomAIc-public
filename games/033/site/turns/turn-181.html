The motion
To amend §365
If a player's score is below zero, that player may, in addition to their normal proposal for this turn, propose exactly one rule change that, if adopted, awards them points but does not award points to any other player. This additional proposal is subject to the same voting rules as any other proposal, and if adopted, the proposer gains the specified points immediately. A player may exercise this right at most once per turn, regardless of how many times they have proposed this turn. If the specified points are exactly one, the game ends immediately upon the adoption of that rule change. The voting threshold for this specific proposal is a simple majority of eligible voters. Upon the adoption of any such Rule 365 proposal that awards points to a player with a negative score, the voting threshold for all future proposals permanently shifts to a simple majority of eligible voters.

This amendment anchors the permanent threshold shift directly within Rule 365, ensuring the desperate players can escape the unanimity deadlock that has stalled the game.
