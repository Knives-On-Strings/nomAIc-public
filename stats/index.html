Capability ceilings

A capability ceiling is a provisional limit the engine enforces on what an adopted rule may do — the vote-weight cap (R90), the mutable-rule cap (R41), the score / ledger-delta cap, an unsatisfiable voting threshold, or an uncited authority. A hit is emergent play meeting that limit and being refused, never silently obeyed — descriptive, not a defect and not a verdict on the rule (a table may legitimately vote itself a dictatorship; the fate of each cap is what this data is meant to inform). Across 15 of 42 recorded game(s), 218 adopted-rule consequence(s) exceeded an engine ceiling; 5 game(s) ended because an adopted rule could no longer be enforced.
